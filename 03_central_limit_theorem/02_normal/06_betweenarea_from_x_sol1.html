First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(I\) axis by adding integer multiples of 8 to 27. We know to shade the between \(15\) and \(39\) because those values satisfy the condition \(15 < I < 39\).


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{i_1-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{15-27}{8} \\\\ &= -1.5 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{i_2-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{39-27}{8} \\\\ &= 1.5 \end{aligned}\]

We rephrase our problem into a standard normal probability problem:

\[P\left(15 < I < 39\right) ~=~ P(-1.5<Z<1.5)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(-1.5<Z<1.5)\).

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(z_1 < Z < z_2) &= P(Z<z_2) - P(Z<z1) \\ P(-1.5<Z<1.5) &= P(Z<1.5)-P(Z<-1.5)\\ &= 0.9332-0.0668 \\ &= 0.8664 \end{aligned}\]
