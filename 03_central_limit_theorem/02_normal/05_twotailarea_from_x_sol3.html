First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(X\) axis by adding integer multiples of 20 to 200. We know to shade the two tails because values far from 200 satisfy the condition \(\big|X-200\big| > 10\). We draw the boundaries at \(200-10=190\) and \(200+10=210\) because those are the solutions to \(\big|X-200\big| = 10\). We can also rephrase the probability.

\[P\left(\big|X-200\big| > 10\right) ~=~ P\big(X<190 \text{ OR } X>210\big)\]


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{190-200}{20} \\\\ &= \frac{-10}{20} \\\\ &= -0.5 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{210-200}{20} \\\\ &= \frac{10}{20} \\\\ &= 0.5 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because

\[P\left(\big|X-200\big| > 10\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big|>0.5\right)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(|Z|>0.5)\). I will also point out that \(\frac{10}{20} = 0.5\). In general, if \(X\) is normally distributed, then: \[P\left(\big|X-\mu\big|>d\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big| > \frac{d}{\sigma}\right)\]

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(|Z|>0.5) &= 2- 2 \times P(Z<0.5) \\ &= 2 - 2 \times 0.6915 \\ &= 0.617 \end{aligned}\]
