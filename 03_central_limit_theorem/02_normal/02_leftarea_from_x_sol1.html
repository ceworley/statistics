First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(A\) axis by adding integer multiples of 4 to 42. We know to shade toward the left because small values of \(A\) satisfy the condition \(A<40\).


We are given a specific \(a\) value as a boundary. (Remember, for random variables we use uppercase letters, but for specific values we use lowercase.) \[a = 40 \] We calculate the \(z\) value of the boundary. \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{a-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{40-42}{4} \\\\ &= -0.5 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because \[P(A<40) ~=~ P(Z<-0.5) \]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(Z<-0.5)\). To do this, you just need a \(z\)-table.

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)
-0.52 0.3015
-0.51 0.305
-0.5 0.3085
-0.49 0.3121
-0.48 0.3156

Thus, we find our answer. \[P(A<40) = 0.3085 \]
