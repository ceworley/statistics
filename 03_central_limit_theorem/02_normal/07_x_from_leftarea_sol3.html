So, we know the \(z\)-score is between 0 and 1. Remember, \(Z\) and \(z\) always refer to the standard normal variable.


By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\) more precisely.

Either 0.49 or 0.5 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=0.4959\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(k\) score. \[\begin{aligned} k &= \mu+z\sigma \\ &= 0.96+(0.4959)(0.24) \\ &= 1.079 \end{aligned}\]
