First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(E\) axis by adding integer multiples of 8 to 79. We know to shade the two tails because values far from 79 satisfy the condition \(\big|E-79\big| > 2\). We draw the boundaries at \(79-2=77\) and \(79+2=81\) because those are the solutions to \(\big|E-79\big| = 2\). We can also rephrase the probability.

\[P\left(\big|E-79\big| > 2\right) ~=~ P\big(E<77 \text{ OR } E>81\big)\]


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{77-79}{8} \\\\ &= \frac{-2}{8} \\\\ &= -0.25 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{81-79}{8} \\\\ &= \frac{2}{8} \\\\ &= 0.25 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because

\[P\left(\big|E-79\big| > 2\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big|>0.25\right)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(|Z|>0.25)\). I will also point out that \(\frac{2}{8} = 0.25\). In general, if \(X\) is normally distributed, then: \[P\left(\big|X-\mu\big|>d\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big| > \frac{d}{\sigma}\right)\]

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(|Z|>0.25) &= 2- 2 \times P(Z<0.25) \\ &= 2 - 2 \times 0.5987 \\ &= 0.8026 \end{aligned}\]
