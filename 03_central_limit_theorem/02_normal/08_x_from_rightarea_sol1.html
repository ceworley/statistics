First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(X\) axis by adding integer multiples of 0.15 to 0.61. We know to shade the right because high values of \(X\) satisfy the condition \(X>x\) (regardless of the exact value of \(x\)). We donâ€™t know exactly where to place the boundary, but we know the right area is 0.94.


We know how to find the left area. \[\begin{aligned} P(X<x) &= 1-P(X>x) \\ &= 1-0.94 \\ &= 0.06 \end{aligned}\]


As an intermediate step, we find \(z\) such that \(P(Z<z)=0.06\).

By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\).

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)
-1.58 0.0571
-1.57 0.0582

Either -1.56 or -1.55 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=-1.5548\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(x\) score. \[\begin{aligned} x &= \mu+z\sigma \\ &= 0.61+(-1.5548)(0.15) \\ &= 0.3768 \end{aligned}\]

We can also visualize this with a spinner.


The tolerance for an acceptable answer was \(\pm 0.01\) from 0.376784. So, anything between 0.366784 and 0.386784 was accepted.
