Random variable \(X\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 0.61\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 0.15\). \[X \sim \text{N}(0.61,\,0.15) \] Evaluate \(x\) such that \(P(X>x) = 0.94\). In other words, determine an lower boundary such that a normal spinner with mean 0.61 and standard deviation 0.15 lands on a value more than that boundary 94% of the time.
