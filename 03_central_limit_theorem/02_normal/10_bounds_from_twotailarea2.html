03_central_limit_theorem/02_normal/10_bounds_from_twotailarea_v2

Random variable \(V\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 0.78\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 0.16\). \[V \sim \text{N}(0.78,\,0.16) \]


  1. Evaluate \(d\) such that \(P\left(\big|V-0.78\big|>d\right) = 0.04\). In other words, determine the distance from mean of two boundaries such that this normal spinner lands beyond those boundaries 4% of the time.
  2. Let \(v_1 = 0.78-d\), such that \(P\left(V < v_1\right) = \frac{0.04}{2}\). Evaluate \(v_1\).
  3. Let \(v_2 = 0.78+d\), such that \(P\left(V > v_2\right) = \frac{0.04}{2}\). Evaluate \(v_2\).
