Random variable \(K\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 0.74\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 0.15\). \[K \sim \text{N}(0.74,\,0.15) \] Evaluate \(k\) such that \(P(K<k) = 0.06\). In other words, determine an upper boundary such that a normal spinner with mean 0.74 and standard deviation 0.15 lands under that boundary 6% of the time.
