First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(G\) axis by adding integer multiples of 180 to 600. We know to shade the right because high values of \(G\) satisfy the condition \(G>g\) (regardless of the exact value of \(g\)). We donâ€™t know exactly where to place the boundary, but we know the right area is 0.22.


We know how to find the left area. \[\begin{aligned} P(G<g) &= 1-P(G>g) \\ &= 1-0.22 \\ &= 0.78 \end{aligned}\]


As an intermediate step, we find \(z\) such that \(P(Z<z)=0.78\).

By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\).

Either 0.77 or 0.78 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=0.7722\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(g\) score. \[\begin{aligned} g &= \mu+z\sigma \\ &= 600+(0.7722)(180) \\ &= 739 \end{aligned}\]

We can also visualize this with a spinner.
