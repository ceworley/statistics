First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(Q\) axis by adding integer multiples of 22 to 93. We know to shade the right because high values of \(Q\) satisfy the condition \(Q>q\) (regardless of the exact value of \(q\)). We donâ€™t know exactly where to place the boundary, but we know the right area is 0.59.


We know how to find the left area. \[\begin{aligned} P(Q<q) &= 1-P(Q>q) \\ &= 1-0.59 \\ &= 0.41 \end{aligned}\]


As an intermediate step, we find \(z\) such that \(P(Z<z)=0.41\).

By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\).

Either -0.23 or -0.22 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=-0.2275\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(q\) score. \[\begin{aligned} q &= \mu+z\sigma \\ &= 93+(-0.2275)(22) \\ &= 87.99 \end{aligned}\]

We can also visualize this with a spinner.
