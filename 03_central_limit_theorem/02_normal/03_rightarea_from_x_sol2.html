First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(E\) axis by adding integer multiples of 40 to 800. We know to shade toward the right because large values of \(E\) satisfy the condition \(E>870\).


We are given a specific \(e\) value as a boundary. (Remember, for random variables we use uppercase letters, but for specific values we use lowercase.) \[e = 870 \] We calculate the \(z\) value of the boundary. \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{e-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{870-800}{40} \\\\ &= 1.75 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because \[P(E>870) ~=~ P(Z>1.75) \]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(Z>1.75)\).

To do this, you need to remember that right-area events are complementary to left-area events. \[P(Z>1.75) ~=~ 1-P(Z<1.75) \]

You can use a \(z\)-table.

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)
1.73 0.9582
1.74 0.9591
1.75 0.9599
1.76 0.9608
1.77 0.9616

Thus, we find our answer. \[\begin{aligned} P(E>870) &= 1-0.9599 \\ &= 0.0401 \end{aligned}\]
