We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (122)(0.47) ~=~ 57.34\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(122)(0.47)(0.53)} ~=~ 5.5127307 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{x_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{51.5-57.34}{5.5127307} \\\\ &= -1.0593661 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{x_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{65.5-57.34}{5.5127307} \\\\ &= 1.4802102 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(-1.0593661 < Z < 1.4802102) ~=~ 0.7858749 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(-1.06 < Z < 1.48) ~=~ 0.786 \] \[P(-1.05 < Z < 1.49) ~=~ 0.785 \] \[P(-1.06 < Z < 1.49) ~=~ 0.7837 \] \[P(-1.05 < Z < 1.48) ~=~ 0.7873 \]
