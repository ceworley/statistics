We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 80.16 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{2.16}{\sqrt{89}} = 0.2289595\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 80.16 and a standard deviation of 0.2289595. \[\bar{X} \sim N(80.16,\,0.2289595) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{0.14}{0.2289595} = 0.6114617 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.61\) or \(z=0.62\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| < 0.6114617) = 0.4591061 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.61) = 0.4582 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.62) = 0.4648 \]
