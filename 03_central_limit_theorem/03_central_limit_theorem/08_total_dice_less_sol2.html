We calculate the mean and standard deviation of the random sum using the formulas from the central limit theorem. \[\begin{aligned} \mu_{ \sum X} &= n\mu_{ X} \\ &= (289)(3.5) \\ &= 1011.5 \end{aligned}\]

\[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{ \sum X} &= \sigma_{ X} \sqrt{n} \\ &= (1.7078251)\sqrt{289} \\ &= 29.0330272 \end{aligned}\]

The central limit theorem tells us that the random sum is approximately normal with the parameters calculated above.

\[\sum X \sim N(1011.5,\,29.0330272) \]

In other words, we can approximate the summing of 289 rolls of 6-sided dice with a single spin of the following spinner.


Find the appropriate \(z\) score.

\[z ~=~ \frac{(\sum x)-\mu_{ \sum X}}{\sigma_{ \sum X}} ~=~ \frac{1037.5-1011.5}{29.0330272} ~=~ 0.8955318\]

You can round this to either 0.89 or 0.9. I will continue with the unrounded \(z\) score. We now rephrase the question as a standard normal probability.

\[P\left(\sum X < 1037.5\right) ~=~ P(Z<0.8955318) \] \[P(Z<0.8955318) ~=~ \, ?\]

You can use the \(z\) table to find the probability.

\[P(Z<0.8955318) ~=~ 0.8147486\]

\[P(Z<0.89) ~=~ 0.8133\] \[P(Z<0.9) ~=~ 0.8159\]
