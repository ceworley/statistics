We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (180)(0.06) ~=~ 10.8\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(180)(0.06)(0.94)} ~=~ 3.1862203 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{x_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{6.5-10.8}{3.1862203} \\\\ &= -1.3495614 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{x_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{9.5-10.8}{3.1862203} \\\\ &= -0.4080069 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(-1.3495614 < Z < -0.4080069) ~=~ 0.2530559 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(-1.35 < Z < -0.41) ~=~ 0.2524 \] \[P(-1.34 < Z < -0.4) ~=~ 0.2545 \] \[P(-1.35 < Z < -0.4) ~=~ 0.2508 \] \[P(-1.34 < Z < -0.41) ~=~ 0.2561 \]
