We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (76)(0.38) ~=~ 28.88\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(76)(0.38)(0.62)} ~=~ 4.2315009 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{26.5-28.88}{4.2315009} \\\\ &= -0.5624482 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < -0.5624482) ~=~ 0.2869053 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < -0.57) ~=~ 0.2843 \] \[P(Z < -0.56) ~=~ 0.2877 \]
