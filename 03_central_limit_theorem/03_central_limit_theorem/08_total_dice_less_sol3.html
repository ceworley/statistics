We calculate the mean and standard deviation of the random sum using the formulas from the central limit theorem. \[\begin{aligned} \mu_{ \sum X} &= n\mu_{ X} \\ &= (361)(3.5) \\ &= 1263.5 \end{aligned}\]

\[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{ \sum X} &= \sigma_{ X} \sqrt{n} \\ &= (1.7078251)\sqrt{361} \\ &= 32.4486774 \end{aligned}\]

The central limit theorem tells us that the random sum is approximately normal with the parameters calculated above.

\[\sum X \sim N(1263.5,\,32.4486774) \]

In other words, we can approximate the summing of 361 rolls of 6-sided dice with a single spin of the following spinner.


Find the appropriate \(z\) score.

\[z ~=~ \frac{(\sum x)-\mu_{ \sum X}}{\sigma_{ \sum X}} ~=~ \frac{1273.5-1263.5}{32.4486774} ~=~ 0.308179\]

You can round this to either 0.3 or 0.31. I will continue with the unrounded \(z\) score. We now rephrase the question as a standard normal probability.

\[P\left(\sum X < 1273.5\right) ~=~ P(Z<0.308179) \] \[P(Z<0.308179) ~=~ \, ?\]

You can use the \(z\) table to find the probability.

\[P(Z<0.308179) ~=~ 0.6210269\]

\[P(Z<0.3) ~=~ 0.6179\] \[P(Z<0.31) ~=~ 0.6217\]
