We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (131)(0.41) ~=~ 53.71\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(131)(0.41)(0.59)} ~=~ 5.6292895 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{55.5-53.71}{5.6292895} \\\\ &= 0.3179797 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z > 0.3179797) ~=~ 0.3752502 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z > 0.31) ~=~ 0.3783 \] \[P(Z > 0.32) ~=~ 0.3745 \]
