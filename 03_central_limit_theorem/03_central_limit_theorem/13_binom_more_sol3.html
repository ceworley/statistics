We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (165)(0.72) ~=~ 118.8\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(165)(0.72)(0.28)} ~=~ 5.7674951 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{120.5-118.8}{5.7674951} \\\\ &= 0.2947553 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z > 0.2947553) ~=~ 0.3840904 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z > 0.29) ~=~ 0.3859 \] \[P(Z > 0.3) ~=~ 0.3821 \]
