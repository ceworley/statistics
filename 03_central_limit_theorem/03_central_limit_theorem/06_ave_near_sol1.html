We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 39.88 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{2.5}{\sqrt{108}} = 0.2405626\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 39.88 and a standard deviation of 0.2405626. \[\bar{X} \sim N(39.88,\,0.2405626) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{0.12}{0.2405626} = 0.4988306 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.49\) or \(z=0.5\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| < 0.4988306) = 0.3821013 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.49) = 0.3758 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.5) = 0.383 \]
