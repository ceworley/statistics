We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 30.31 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{3.34}{\sqrt{84}} = 0.3644239\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 30.31 and a standard deviation of 0.3644239. \[\bar{X} \sim N(30.31,\,0.3644239) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{0.66}{0.3644239} = 1.8110778 \]

You can round that to \(z=1.81\) or \(z=1.82\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| < 1.8110778) = 0.9298712 \] \[P(|Z| < 1.81) = 0.9298 \] \[P(|Z| < 1.82) = 0.9312 \]
