We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.16\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.16)(0.84)}{63}} ~=~ 0.046188 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.1032-0.16}{0.046188} \\\\ &= -1.2303059 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{\hat{p}_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.1825-0.16}{0.046188} \\\\ &= 0.4879984 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(-1.2303059 < Z < 0.4879984) ~=~ 0.5779332 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(-1.24 < Z < 0.48) ~=~ 0.5769 \] \[P(-1.23 < Z < 0.49) ~=~ 0.5786 \] \[P(-1.24 < Z < 0.49) ~=~ 0.5751 \] \[P(-1.23 < Z < 0.48) ~=~ 0.5804 \]
