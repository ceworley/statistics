We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (30)(44.38) ~=~ 1331.4 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (1.75)\sqrt{30} = 9.5851448\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 1331.4 and a standard deviation of 9.5851448. \[\sum{X} \sim N(1331.4,\,9.5851448) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{8.6}{9.5851448} = 0.8972217 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.89\) or \(z=0.9\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| > 0.8972217) = 0.3696006 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.89) = 0.3734 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.9) = 0.3682 \]
