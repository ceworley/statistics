We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.9\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.9)(0.1)}{196}} ~=~ 0.0214286 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.8852-0.9}{0.0214286} \\\\ &= -0.6904762 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{\hat{p}_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.8954-0.9}{0.0214286} \\\\ &= -0.2142857 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(-0.6904762 < Z < -0.2142857) ~=~ 0.1702147 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(-0.7 < Z < -0.22) ~=~ 0.1709 \] \[P(-0.69 < Z < -0.21) ~=~ 0.1717 \] \[P(-0.7 < Z < -0.21) ~=~ 0.1678 \] \[P(-0.69 < Z < -0.22) ~=~ 0.1748 \]
