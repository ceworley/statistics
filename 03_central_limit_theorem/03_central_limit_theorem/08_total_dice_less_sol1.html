We calculate the mean and standard deviation of the random sum using the formulas from the central limit theorem. \[\begin{aligned} \mu_{ \sum X} &= n\mu_{ X} \\ &= (256)(10.5) \\ &= 2688 \end{aligned}\]

\[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{ \sum X} &= \sigma_{ X} \sqrt{n} \\ &= (5.7662813)\sqrt{256} \\ &= 92.2605008 \end{aligned}\]

The central limit theorem tells us that the random sum is approximately normal with the parameters calculated above.

\[\sum X \sim N(2688,\,92.2605008) \]

In other words, we can approximate the summing of 256 rolls of 20-sided dice with a single spin of the following spinner.


Find the appropriate \(z\) score.

\[z ~=~ \frac{(\sum x)-\mu_{ \sum X}}{\sigma_{ \sum X}} ~=~ \frac{2818.5-2688}{92.2605008} ~=~ 1.4144731\]

You can round this to either 1.41 or 1.42. I will continue with the unrounded \(z\) score. We now rephrase the question as a standard normal probability.

\[P\left(\sum X < 2818.5\right) ~=~ P(Z<1.4144731) \] \[P(Z<1.4144731) ~=~ \, ?\]

You can use the \(z\) table to find the probability.

\[P(Z<1.4144731) ~=~ 0.9213885\]

\[P(Z<1.41) ~=~ 0.9207\] \[P(Z<1.42) ~=~ 0.9222\]
