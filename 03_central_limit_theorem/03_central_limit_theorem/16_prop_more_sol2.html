We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.58\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.58)(0.42)}{n}} ~=~ 0.062682 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (The common notation is \(\hat{p}\) for a specific proportion.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.6371-0.58}{0.062682} \\\\ &= 0.9109474 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z > 0.9109474) ~=~ 0.1811615 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z > 0.91) ~=~ 0.1814 \] \[P(Z > 0.92) ~=~ 0.1788 \]
