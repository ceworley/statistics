We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 35.08 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{3.18}{\sqrt{127}} = 0.2821794\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 35.08 and a standard deviation of 0.2821794. \[\bar{X} \sim N(35.08,\,0.2821794) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{0.16}{0.2821794} = 0.5670152 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.56\) or \(z=0.57\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| > 0.5670152) = 0.5707038 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.56) = 0.5754 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.57) = 0.5686 \]
