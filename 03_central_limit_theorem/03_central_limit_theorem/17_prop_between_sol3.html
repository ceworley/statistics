We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.33\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.33)(0.67)}{169}} ~=~ 0.0361702 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.2988-0.33}{0.0361702} \\\\ &= -0.8621303 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{\hat{p}_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.358-0.33}{0.0361702} \\\\ &= 0.7737905 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(-0.8621303 < Z < 0.7737905) ~=~ 0.5861647 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(-0.87 < Z < 0.77) ~=~ 0.5872 \] \[P(-0.86 < Z < 0.78) ~=~ 0.5874 \] \[P(-0.87 < Z < 0.78) ~=~ 0.5845 \] \[P(-0.86 < Z < 0.77) ~=~ 0.5901 \]
