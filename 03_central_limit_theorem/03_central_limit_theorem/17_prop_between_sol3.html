We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.09\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.09)(0.91)}{165}} ~=~ 0.0222792 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.1061-0.09}{0.0222792} \\\\ &= 0.7208783 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{\hat{p}_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.1182-0.09}{0.0222792} \\\\ &= 1.2649374 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(0.7208783 < Z < 1.2649374) ~=~ 0.1325453 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(0.72 < Z < 1.26) ~=~ 0.132 \] \[P(0.73 < Z < 1.27) ~=~ 0.1307 \] \[P(0.72 < Z < 1.27) ~=~ 0.1289 \] \[P(0.73 < Z < 1.26) ~=~ 0.1338 \]
