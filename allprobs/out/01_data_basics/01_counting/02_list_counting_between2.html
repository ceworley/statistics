01_data_basics/01_counting/02_list_counting_between_v2

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

\[\mathbf{x} = 54, 55, 59, 55, 53, 51, 57, 59, 55, 53 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_between_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are inside 52.5 to 55.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}>52.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<55.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[52.5<\mathbf{x}<55.5]\).
  2. How many of the measurements are outside 50.5 to 51.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}<50.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>51.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[\texttt{NOT}(50.5<\mathbf{x}<51.5)]\).
  3. How many of the measurements are closer than 3 units from 56.5? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-56.5\big|<3\right]\).
  4. How many of the measurements are farther than 4 units from 54.5? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-54.5\big|>4\right]\).
  5. Determine an integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 54.5 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-54.5\big|<r\right] ~=~ 6\).
  6. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 56 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-56\big|>r\right] ~=~ 6\).
