01_data_basics/01_counting/02_list_counting_between_v2

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

\[\mathbf{x} = 87, 87, 85, 88, 85, 88, 84, 81, 88, 81 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_between_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are inside 80.5 to 83.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}>80.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<83.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[80.5<\mathbf{x}<83.5]\).
  2. How many of the measurements are outside 83.5 to 89.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}<83.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>89.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[\texttt{NOT}(83.5<\mathbf{x}<89.5)]\).
  3. How many of the measurements are closer than 1.5 units from 83? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-83\big|<1.5\right]\).
  4. How many of the measurements are farther than 4 units from 85.5? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-85.5\big|>4\right]\).
  5. Determine an integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 82.5 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-82.5\big|<r\right] ~=~ 3\).
  6. Determine an integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 83.5 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-83.5\big|>r\right] ~=~ 5\).
