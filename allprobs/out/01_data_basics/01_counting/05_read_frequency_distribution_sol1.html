The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.

  2. (ANS=122) There are 122 measurements more than 54. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>54]=122\).
  3. (ANS=53) There are 53 measurements closer than 1.5 units from 59.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-59.5|<1.5]=53\).
  4. (ANS=34) There are 34 measurements further than 3 units from 56. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-56|>3]=34\).
  5. (ANS=58) A boundary at \(b=58\) has 73 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<58]=73\).
  6. (ANS=57) A boundary at \(b=57\) has 71 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>57]=71\).
  7. (ANS=4) An interval with radius \(r=4\) around center 57 contains 125 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-57|<4]=125\).
  8. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 58.5 excludes 106 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-58.5|>0.5]=106\).
