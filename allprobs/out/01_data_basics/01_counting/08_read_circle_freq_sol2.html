The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=46) There are 46 measurements less than 3.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<3.5]=46\).
  2. (ANS=54) There are 54 measurements more than 3.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>3.5]=54\).
  3. (ANS=23) There are 23 measurements closer than 1 units from 2.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-2.5|<1]=23\).
  4. (ANS=62) There are 62 measurements further than 1.5 units from 3. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|>1.5]=62\).
  5. (ANS=4.5) A boundary at \(b=4.5\) has 61 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<4.5]=61\).
  6. (ANS=1.5) A boundary at \(b=1.5\) has 77 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>1.5]=77\).
  7. (ANS=2) An interval with radius \(r=2\) around center 3.5 contains 56 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|<2]=56\).
  8. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 3 excludes 89 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|>0.5]=89\).
