The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=14) There are 14 measurements less than 30. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<30]=14\).
  2. (ANS=67) There are 67 measurements more than 35. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>35]=67\).
  3. (ANS=116) There are 116 measurements closer than 10 units from 35. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-35|<10]=116\).
  4. (ANS=107) There are 107 measurements further than 2.5 units from 27.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-27.5|>2.5]=107\).
  5. (ANS=30) A boundary at \(b=30\) has 14 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<30]=14\).
  6. (ANS=40) A boundary at \(b=40\) has 21 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>40]=21\).
  7. (ANS=7.5) An interval with radius \(r=7.5\) around center 37.5 contains 103 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-37.5|<7.5]=103\).
  8. (ANS=5) An interval with radius \(r=5\) around center 40 excludes 56 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-40|>5]=56\).
