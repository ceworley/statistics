The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=38) There are 38 measurements less than 80. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<80]=38\).
  2. (ANS=62) There are 62 measurements more than 70. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>70]=62\).
  3. (ANS=7) There are 7 measurements closer than 2.5 units from 72.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-72.5|<2.5]=7\).
  4. (ANS=70) There are 70 measurements further than 7.5 units from 77.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-77.5|>7.5]=70\).
  5. (ANS=75) A boundary at \(b=75\) has 35 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<75]=35\).
  6. (ANS=80) A boundary at \(b=80\) has 52 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>80]=52\).
  7. (ANS=2.5) An interval with radius \(r=2.5\) around center 77.5 contains 3 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-77.5|<2.5]=3\).
  8. (ANS=5) An interval with radius \(r=5\) around center 75 excludes 80 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-75|>5]=80\).
