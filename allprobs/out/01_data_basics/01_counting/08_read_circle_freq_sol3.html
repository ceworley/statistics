The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=69) There are 69 measurements less than 4.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<4.5]=69\).
  2. (ANS=83) There are 83 measurements more than 1.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>1.5]=83\).
  3. (ANS=17) There are 17 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-5|<0.5]=17\).
  4. (ANS=70) There are 70 measurements further than 1 units from 3.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|>1]=70\).
  5. (ANS=5.5) A boundary at \(b=5.5\) has 86 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<5.5]=86\).
  6. (ANS=1.5) A boundary at \(b=1.5\) has 83 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>1.5]=83\).
  7. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 2 contains 22 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-2|<0.5]=22\).
  8. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 3 excludes 86 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|>0.5]=86\).
