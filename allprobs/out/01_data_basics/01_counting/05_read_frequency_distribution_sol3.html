The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=96) There are 96 measurements less than 85. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<85]=96\).
  2. (ANS=66) There are 66 measurements more than 80. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>80]=66\).
  3. (ANS=37) There are 37 measurements closer than 2.5 units from 82.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-82.5|<2.5]=37\).
  4. (ANS=59) There are 59 measurements further than 5 units from 80. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-80|>5]=59\).
  5. (ANS=85) A boundary at \(b=85\) has 96 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<85]=96\).
  6. (ANS=80) A boundary at \(b=80\) has 66 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>80]=66\).
  7. (ANS=2.5) An interval with radius \(r=2.5\) around center 77.5 contains 29 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-77.5|<2.5]=29\).
  8. (ANS=5) An interval with radius \(r=5\) around center 80 excludes 59 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-80|>5]=59\).
