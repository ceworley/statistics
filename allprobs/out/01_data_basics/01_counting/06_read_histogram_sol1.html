The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=13) There are 13 measurements less than 51. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<51]=13\).
  2. (ANS=54) There are 54 measurements more than 51.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>51.5]=54\).
  3. (ANS=28) There are 28 measurements closer than 0.75 units from 52.25. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-52.25|<0.75]=28\).
  4. (ANS=73) There are 73 measurements further than 0.25 units from 53.25. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-53.25|>0.25]=73\).
  5. (ANS=52) A boundary at \(b=52\) has 36 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<52]=36\).
  6. (ANS=51.5) A boundary at \(b=51.5\) has 54 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>51.5]=54\).
  7. (ANS=2) An interval with radius \(r=2\) around center 52.5 contains 67 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-52.5|<2]=67\).
  8. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 52.5 excludes 22 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-52.5|>1.5]=22\).
