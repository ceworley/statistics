The first 4 questions involve adding up relative frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=0.3 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 30 measurements less than 48.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<48.5]=\frac{30}{100}=0.3\).
  2. (ANS=0.82 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 82 measurements more than 47.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>47.5]=\frac{82}{100}=0.82\).
  3. (ANS=0.27 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 27 measurements closer than 1.25 units from 47.25. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-47.25|<1.25]=0.27\).
  4. (ANS=0.49 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 49 measurements further than 1.5 units from 48. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-48|>1.5]=0.49\).
  5. (ANS=48) First, \(0.23 \cdot 100 = 23\). A boundary at \(b=48\) has 23 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<48]=0.23\).
  6. (ANS=47.5) First, \(0.82 \cdot 100 = 82\). A boundary at \(b=47.5\) has 82 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>47.5]=0.82\).
  7. (ANS=1.25) First, \(0.49 \cdot 100 = 49\). An interval with radius \(r=1.25\) around center 48.25 contains 49 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-48.25|<1.25]=0.49\).
  8. (ANS=0.25) First, \(0.83 \cdot 100 = 83\). An interval with radius \(r=0.25\) around center 49.25 excludes 83 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-49.25|<0.25]=0.83\).
