01_data_basics/02_proportion/02_list_proportion_between_v3

The lengths (in centimeters) of 10 lizards were recorded.

\[\mathbf{x} = 38, 39, 33, 36, 32, 34, 39, 37, 32, 37 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): lizard_data.csv


  1. What proportion of the measurements are inside 33.5 cm to 36.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>33.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<36.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[33.5<\mathbf{x}<36.5]\).
  2. What proportion of the measurements are outside 33.5 cm to 35.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<33.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>35.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(33.5<\mathbf{x}<35.5)]\).
  3. What proportion of the measurements are closer than 1 cm from 32.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-32.5\big|<1\right]\).
  4. What proportion of the measurements are farther than 1 cm from 36.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-36.5\big|>1\right]\).
  5. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 36 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-36\big|<r\right] ~=~ 0.8\).
  6. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 38 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-38\big|>r\right] ~=~ 0.9\).
