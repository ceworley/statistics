The first 4 questions involve adding up relative frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (0.6) The proportion of measurements less than 3.5 is 0.6. \[\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<3.5]=0.6\]
  2. (0.7) The proportion of measurements more than 1.5 is 0.7. \[\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>1.5]=0.7\]
  3. (0.4) The proportion of measurements closer than 1.5 units from 4 is 0.4. \[\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-4|<1.5]=0.4\]
  4. (0.7) The proportion of measurements farther than 1 units from 2.5 is 0.7. \[\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-2.5|>1]=0.7\]
  5. .(4.5) The proportion of measurements less than 4.5 is 0.7. \[\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<4.5]=0.7\]
  6. .(4.5) The proportion of measurements more than 4.5 is 0.3. \[\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>4.5]=0.3\]
  7. .(2) The proportion of measurements closer than 2 units from 3.5 is 0.5. \[\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|<2]=0.5\]
  8. .(0.5) The proportion of measurements farther than 0.5 units from 3 is 0.8. \[\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-3|>0.5]=0.8\]
