You may find it helpful to convert the densities to frequencies by multiplying each density by both the total sample size (\(n=100\)) and the width of the bar (2).


The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=0.93) There are 93 measurements less than 34. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<34]=0.93\).
  2. (ANS=0.17) There are 17 measurements more than 32. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>32]=0.17\).
  3. (ANS=0.06) There are 6 measurements closer than 2 units from 36. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-36|<2]=0.06\).
  4. (ANS=0.88) There are 88 measurements further than 2 units from 30. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-30|>2]=0.88\).
  5. (ANS=36) A boundary at \(b=36\) has 95 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<36]=0.95\).
  6. (ANS=36) A boundary at \(b=36\) has 5 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>36]=0.05\).
  7. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 37 contains 4 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-37|<1]=0.04\).
  8. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 35 excludes 98 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-35|>1]=0.98\).

ANSWERS: 0.93, 0.17, 0.06, 0.88, 36, 36, 1, 1
