The first 4 questions involve adding up relative frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.

You may also find it is helpful to determine frequencies (counts). You do this by multiplying each relative frequency by the total number of measurements.



  1. (ANS=0.62) There are 124 measurements less than 5.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<5.5]=\frac{124}{200}=0.62\).
  2. (ANS=0.64) There are 128 measurements more than 3.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>3.5]=\frac{128}{200}=0.64\).
  3. (ANS=0.49) There are 98 measurements closer than 2 units from 4.5. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-4.5|<2]=\frac{98}{200}=0.49\).
  4. (ANS=0.415) There are 83 measurements further than 2.5 units from 5. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-5|>2.5]=\frac{83}{200}=0.415\).
  5. (ANS=3.5) First, convert the proportion into a count. \(0.36\cdot200=72\). A boundary at \(b=3.5\) has 72 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<3.5]=0.36\).
  6. (ANS=4.5) First, convert the proportion into a count. \(0.53\cdot200=106\). A boundary at \(b=4.5\) has 106 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>4.5]=0.53\).
  7. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 3.5 contains 42 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|<1]=0.21\).
  8. (ANS=2) An interval with radius \(r=2\) around center 5.5 excludes 103 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-5.5|>2]=0.515\).
