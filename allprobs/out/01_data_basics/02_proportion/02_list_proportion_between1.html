01_data_basics/02_proportion/02_list_proportion_between_v1

The lengths (in centimeters) of 10 lizards were recorded.

\[\mathbf{x} = 83, 83, 89, 87, 89, 86, 89, 85, 86, 82 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): lizard_data.csv


  1. What proportion of the measurements are inside 83.5 cm to 84.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>83.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<84.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[83.5<\mathbf{x}<84.5]\).
  2. What proportion of the measurements are outside 84.5 cm to 88.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<84.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>88.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(84.5<\mathbf{x}<88.5)]\).
  3. What proportion of the measurements are closer than 1 cm from 85.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-85.5\big|<1\right]\).
  4. What proportion of the measurements are farther than 3.5 cm from 84 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-84\big|>3.5\right]\).
  5. Determine an integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 86.5 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-86.5\big|<r\right] ~=~ 0.7\).
  6. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 87 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-87\big|>r\right] ~=~ 0.9\).
