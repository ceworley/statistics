01_data_basics/02_proportion/02_list_proportion_between_v2

The lengths (in centimeters) of 10 lizards were recorded.

\[\mathbf{x} = 81, 88, 89, 82, 87, 82, 83, 85, 89, 86 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): lizard_data.csv


  1. What proportion of the measurements are inside 82.5 cm to 89.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>82.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<89.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[82.5<\mathbf{x}<89.5]\).
  2. What proportion of the measurements are outside 85.5 cm to 89.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<85.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>89.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(85.5<\mathbf{x}<89.5)]\).
  3. What proportion of the measurements are closer than 3.5 cm from 84 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-84\big|<3.5\right]\).
  4. What proportion of the measurements are farther than 1 cm from 85.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-85.5\big|>1\right]\).
  5. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 87 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-87\big|<r\right] ~=~ 0.1\).
  6. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 85 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-85\big|>r\right] ~=~ 0.9\).
