The first 4 questions involve adding up relative frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=0.1625 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 13 measurements less than 51. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<51]=\frac{13}{80}=0.1625\).
  2. (ANS=0.675 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 54 measurements more than 51.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>51.5]=\frac{54}{80}=0.675\).
  3. (ANS=0.35 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 28 measurements closer than 0.75 units from 52.25. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-52.25|<0.75]=0.35\).
  4. (ANS=0.9125 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 73 measurements further than 0.25 units from 53.25. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-53.25|>0.25]=0.9125\).
  5. (ANS=52) First, \(0.45 \cdot 80 = 36\). A boundary at \(b=52\) has 36 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<52]=0.45\).
  6. (ANS=51.5) First, \(0.675 \cdot 80 = 54\). A boundary at \(b=51.5\) has 54 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>51.5]=0.675\).
  7. (ANS=2) First, \(0.8375 \cdot 80 = 67\). An interval with radius \(r=2\) around center 52.5 contains 67 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-52.5|<2]=0.8375\).
  8. (ANS=1.5) First, \(0.275 \cdot 80 = 22\). An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 52.5 excludes 22 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-52.5|<1.5]=0.275\).
