To determine the median by hand, you first sort the sample. \[\text{sort}(\mathbf{x})=37.59, 37.62, 38.27, 40.99, 41.76, 43.15, 43.4, 43.67, 43.82, 43.83, 44, 44.96, 44.97\] If the sample size is odd, just find the number in the middle number (the \(\frac{n+1}{2}\)th value). If the sample size is even, determine the mean of the middle two numbers (the \(\frac{n}{2}\)th and \(\left(\frac{n}{2}+1\right)\)th values).


In a spreadsheet, you can use the MEDIAN function. You can see a solution spreadsheet here.

In R, you can use the MEDIAN function.
