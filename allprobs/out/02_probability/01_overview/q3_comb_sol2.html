This scenario describes a combinations problem (order does not matter). We are considering the subsets of size 5 from a set of size 8.

\[\begin{aligned} {_nC_r} &= \frac{n!}{(n-r)! \cdot r!} \\\\ n &= 8 \\\\ r &= 5 \\\\ {_{8}C_{5}} &= \frac{8!}{(8-5)!\cdot 5! } \\\\ &= \frac{8!}{3! \cdot 5!} \\\\ &= \frac{ 8\cdot7\cdot6\cdot5\cdot4}{ 5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1} \\\\ &= 56 \end{aligned}\]
