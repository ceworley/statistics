This is a binomial distribution, so use the appropriate formulas. \[\mathbb{P}[X=x] ~=~ _n\text{C}_x \cdot p^x(1-p)^{n-x}\] where \(p\) is the probability of success on each trial, \(x\) is a specific number of successes, \(n\) is the number of trials, and \(C\) is the combinations operator (so that \({_n}C_x = \frac{n!}{(n-x)! \, x!}\)). Some people prefer to also use \(q\) as the probability of failure, such that \(q=1-p\). \[\mathbb{P}[X=x] ~=~ _n\text{C}_x \cdot p^xq^{n-x}\]
