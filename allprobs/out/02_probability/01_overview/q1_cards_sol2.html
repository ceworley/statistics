The key logical terms are “and”, “or”, and “given”. Notice that I am using “given” as a shorter version of “under the condition”.


  1. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{bike and indigo}]=\frac{14}{342}=0.0409\)
  2. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{shovel given indigo}]=\frac{43}{119}=0.361\)
  3. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{mop}]=\frac{73}{342}=0.213\)
  4. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{indigo given bike}] = 0.241\) and \(\mathbb{P}[\text{indigo given mop}] = 0.315\), so a mop is more likely to be indigo than a bike is. / .
  5. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{teal given mop}]=\frac{35}{73}=0.479\)
  6. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{teal}]=\frac{155}{342}=0.453\)
  7. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{mop or indigo}]=\frac{73+119-23}{342}=0.494\)

ANSWERS: 0.0409, 0.361, 0.213, c(FALSE, TRUE), 0.479, 0.453, 0.494
TOLERANCES: 0.01, 0.01, 0.01, 0.01, 0.01, 0.01, 0.01
