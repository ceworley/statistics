The key logical terms are “and”, “or”, and “given”. Notice that I am using “given” as a shorter version of “under the condition”.


  1. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{bike given violet}]=\frac{19}{85}=0.224\)
  2. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{dog}]=\frac{136}{440}=0.309\)
  3. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{violet}]=\frac{85}{440}=0.193\)
  4. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{black given bike}] = 0.157\) and \(\mathbb{P}[\text{black given wheel}] = 0.484\), so a wheel is more likely to be black than a bike is. / .
  5. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{bike or red}]=\frac{108+97-29}{440}=0.4\)
  6. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{orange given wheel}]=\frac{16}{95}=0.168\)
  7. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{flower and red}]=\frac{13}{440}=0.0295\)
