The key logical terms are “and”, “or”, and “given”. Notice that I am using “given” as a shorter version of “under the condition”.


  1. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{flower and pink}]=\frac{15}{241}=0.0622\)
  2. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{pink given pig}]=\frac{43}{82}=0.524\)
  3. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{green}]=\frac{55}{241}=0.228\)
  4. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{house}]=\frac{88}{241}=0.365\)
  5. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{white given flower}] = 0.507\) and \(\mathbb{P}[\text{white given pig}] = 0.317\), so a flower is more likely to be white than a pig is. / .
  6. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{pig given pink}]=\frac{43}{100}=0.43\)
  7. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{flower or pink}]=\frac{71+100-15}{241}=0.647\)

ANSWERS: 0.0622, 0.524, 0.228, 0.365, c(TRUE, FALSE), 0.43, 0.647
