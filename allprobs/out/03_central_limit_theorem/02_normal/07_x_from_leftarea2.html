Random variable \(W\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 310\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 90\). \[W \sim \text{N}(310,\,90) \] Evaluate \(w\) such that \(P(W<w) = 0.09\). In other words, determine an upper boundary such that a normal spinner with mean 310 and standard deviation 90 lands under that boundary 9% of the time.
