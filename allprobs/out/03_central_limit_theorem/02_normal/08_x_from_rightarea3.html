Random variable \(Q\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 93\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 22\). \[Q \sim \text{N}(93,\,22) \] Evaluate \(q\) such that \(P(Q>q) = 0.59\). In other words, determine an lower boundary such that a normal spinner with mean 93 and standard deviation 22 lands above that boundary 59% of the time.
