Random variable \(F\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 2.1\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 0.5\). \[F = \text{N}(2.1,\,0.5) \] Evaluate \(f\) such that \(P(F<f) = 0.17\). In other words, determine an upper boundary such that a normal spinner with mean 2.1 and standard deviation 0.5 lands below that boundary 17% of the time.
