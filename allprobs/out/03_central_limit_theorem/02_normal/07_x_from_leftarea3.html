Random variable \(K\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 0.96\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 0.24\). \[K \sim \text{N}(0.96,\,0.24) \] Evaluate \(k\) such that \(P(K<k) = 0.69\). In other words, determine an upper boundary such that a normal spinner with mean 0.96 and standard deviation 0.24 lands below that boundary 69% of the time.
