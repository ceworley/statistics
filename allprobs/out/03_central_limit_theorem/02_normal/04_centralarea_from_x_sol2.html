First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(B\) axis by adding integer multiples of 50 to 460. We know to shade the center because values near 460 satisfy the condition \(\big|B-460\big| < 70\). We draw the boundaries at \(460-70=390\) and \(460+70=530\) because those are the solutions to \(\big|B-460\big| = 70\). We can also rephrase the probability.

\[P\left(\big|B-460\big| < 70\right) ~=~ P(390<B<530)\]


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{390-460}{50} \\\\ &= \frac{-70}{50} \\\\ &= -1.4 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{530-460}{50} \\\\ &= \frac{70}{50} \\\\ &= 1.4 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because

\[P\left(\big|B-460\big| < 70\right) ~=~ P(390<B<530) ~=~ P(-1.4<Z<1.4) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big|<1.4\right)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(|Z|<1.4)\). I will also point out that \(\frac{70}{50} = 1.4\). In general, if \(X\) is normally distributed, then: \[P\left(\big|X-\mu\big|<d\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big| < \frac{d}{\sigma}\right)\]

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(|Z|<1.4) &= 2 \times P(Z<1.4)-1 \\ &= 2 \times 0.9192-1 \\ &= 0.8384 \end{aligned}\]
