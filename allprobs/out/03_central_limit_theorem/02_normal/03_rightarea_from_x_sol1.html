First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(A\) axis by adding integer multiples of 50 to 250. We know to shade toward the right because large values of \(A\) satisfy the condition \(A>160\).


We are given a specific \(a\) value as a boundary. (Remember, for random variables we use uppercase letters, but for specific values we use lowercase.) \[a = 160 \] We calculate the \(z\) value of the boundary. \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{a-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{160-250}{50} \\\\ &= -1.8 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because \[P(A>160) ~=~ P(Z>-1.8) \]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(Z>-1.8)\).

To do this, you need to remember that right-area events are complementary to left-area events. \[P(Z>-1.8) ~=~ 1-P(Z<-1.8) \]

You can use a \(z\)-table.

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)
-1.82 0.0344
-1.81 0.0351
-1.8 0.0359
-1.79 0.0367
-1.78 0.0375

Thus, we find our answer. \[\begin{aligned} P(A>160) &= 1-0.0359 \\ &= 0.9641 \end{aligned}\]
