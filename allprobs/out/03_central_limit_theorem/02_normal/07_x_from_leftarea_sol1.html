So, we know the \(z\)-score is between -2 and -1. Remember, \(Z\) and \(z\) always refer to the standard normal variable.


By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\) more precisely.

Either -1.56 or -1.55 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=-1.5548\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(k\) score. \[\begin{aligned} k &= \mu+z\sigma \\ &= 0.74+(-1.5548)(0.15) \\ &= 0.5068 \end{aligned}\]
