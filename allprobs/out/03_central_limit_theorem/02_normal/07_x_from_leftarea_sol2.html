So, we know the \(z\)-score is between -2 and -1. Remember, \(Z\) and \(z\) always refer to the standard normal variable.


By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\) more precisely.

Either -1.35 or -1.34 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=-1.3408\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(w\) score. \[\begin{aligned} w &= \mu+z\sigma \\ &= 310+(-1.3408)(90) \\ &= 189.3 \end{aligned}\]
