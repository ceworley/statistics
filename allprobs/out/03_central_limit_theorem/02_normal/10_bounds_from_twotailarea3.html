03_central_limit_theorem/02_normal/10_bounds_from_twotailarea_v3

Random variable \(F\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 2.3\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 0.5\). \[F \sim \text{N}(2.3,\,0.5) \]


  1. Evaluate \(d\) such that \(P\left(\big|F-2.3\big|>d\right) = 0.56\). In other words, determine the distance from mean of two boundaries such that this normal spinner lands beyond those boundaries 56% of the time.
  2. Let \(f_1 = 2.3-d\), such that \(P\left(F < f_1\right) = \frac{0.56}{2}\). Evaluate \(f_1\).
  3. Let \(f_2 = 2.3+d\), such that \(P\left(F > f_2\right) = \frac{0.56}{2}\). Evaluate \(f_2\).
