First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(E\) axis by adding integer multiples of 160 to 750. We know to shade the between \(670\) and \(910\) because those values satisfy the condition \(670 < E < 910\).


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{e_1-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{670-750}{160} \\\\ &= -0.5 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{e_2-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{910-750}{160} \\\\ &= 1 \end{aligned}\]

We rephrase our problem into a standard normal probability problem:

\[P\left(670 < E < 910\right) ~=~ P(-0.5<Z<1)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(-0.5<Z<1)\).

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(z_1 < Z < z_2) &= P(Z<z_2) - P(Z<z1) \\ P(-0.5<Z<1) &= P(Z<1)-P(Z<-0.5)\\ &= 0.8413-0.3085 \\ &= 0.5328 \end{aligned}\]
