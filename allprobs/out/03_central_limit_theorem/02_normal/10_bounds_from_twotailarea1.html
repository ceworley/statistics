03_central_limit_theorem/02_normal/10_bounds_from_twotailarea_v1

Random variable \(C\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 480\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 150\). \[C = \text{N}(480,\,150) \]


  1. Evaluate \(d\) such that \(P\left(\big|C-480\big|>d\right) = 0.66\). In other words, determine the distance from mean of two boundaries such that this normal spinner lands outside those boundaries 66% of the time.
  2. Let \(c_1 = 480-d\), such that \(P\left(C < c_1\right) = \frac{0.66}{2}\). Evaluate \(c_1\).
  3. Let \(c_2 = 480+d\), such that \(P\left(C > c_2\right) = \frac{0.66}{2}\). Evaluate \(c_2\).
