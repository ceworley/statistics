03_central_limit_theorem/02_normal/10_bounds_from_twotailarea_v1

Random variable \(C\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 43\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 13\). \[C \sim \text{N}(43,\,13) \]


  1. Evaluate \(d\) such that \(P\left(\big|C-43\big|>d\right) = 0.24\). In other words, determine the distance from mean of two boundaries such that this normal spinner lands beyond those boundaries 24% of the time.
  2. Let \(c_1 = 43-d\), such that \(P\left(C < c_1\right) = \frac{0.24}{2}\). Evaluate \(c_1\).
  3. Let \(c_2 = 43+d\), such that \(P\left(C > c_2\right) = \frac{0.24}{2}\). Evaluate \(c_2\).
