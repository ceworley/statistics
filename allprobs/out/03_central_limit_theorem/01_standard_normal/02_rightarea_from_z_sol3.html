First, you need to identify that we are looking for a right area. This is because large values of \(Z\) satisfy \(Z>-1.46\).


By using the z-table, we find the left area (even though we eventually want the right area).

\[P(Z<-1.46)=0.0721\]

We use the rule of compliments to determine the right area.

\[\begin{aligned} P(Z > -1.46) &= 1-P(Z < -1.46) \\ &= 1-0.0721 \\ &= 0.9279 \end{aligned}\]

Method 2: You might also recognize that the normal distribution is symmetric. Thus,

\[\begin{aligned} P(Z > -1.46) &= P(Z < 1.46) \\ &= 0.9279 \end{aligned}\]
