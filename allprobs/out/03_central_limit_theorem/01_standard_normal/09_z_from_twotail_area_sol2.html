Start with a sketch. The total two-tail area is 0.06, so each tail has half that area.


Method 1: Determine the area of each tail and the center. Both tails have the same area, and all three areas add to 1. Thus, \[P(Z>z) = \frac{0.06}{2} = 0.03\]

\[P(Z<-z) = \frac{0.06}{2} = 0.03\]

\[\begin{aligned} P(|Z|<z) &= 1-P(|Z|>z) \\ &= 1-0.06 \\ &= 0.94 \end{aligned}\]

Find the left area.

\[\begin{aligned} P(Z<z) &= 0.03+0.94 \\ &= 0.97 \end{aligned}\]
