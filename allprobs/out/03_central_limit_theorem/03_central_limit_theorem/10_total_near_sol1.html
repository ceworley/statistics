We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (39)(51.78) ~=~ 2019.42 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (6.06)\sqrt{39} = 37.8446879\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 2019.42 and a standard deviation of 37.8446879. \[\sum{X} \sim N(2019.42,\,37.8446879) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{30.58}{37.8446879} = 0.8080394 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.8\) or \(z=0.81\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| < 0.8080394) = 0.5809321 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.8) = 0.5762 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.81) = 0.582 \]
