We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (86)(74.61) ~=~ 6416.46 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (13.19)\sqrt{86} = 122.319028\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 6416.46 and a standard deviation of 122.319028. \[\sum{X} \sim N(6416.46,\,122.319028) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{43.54}{122.319028} = 0.3559544 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.35\) or \(z=0.36\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| < 0.3559544) = 0.2781253 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.35) = 0.2736 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.36) = 0.2812 \]
