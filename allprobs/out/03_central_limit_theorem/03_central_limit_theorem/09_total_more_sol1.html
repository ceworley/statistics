We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (112)(56.11) ~=~ 6284.32 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (3.28)\sqrt{112} = 34.7122572\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 6284.32 and a standard deviation of 34.7122572. \[\sum{X} \sim N(6284.32,\,34.7122572) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{6290-6284.32}{34.7122572} = 0.163631 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.16\) or \(z=0.17\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z > 0.163631) = 0.4350108 \] \[P(Z > 0.16) = 0.4364 \] \[P(Z > 0.17) = 0.4325 \]
