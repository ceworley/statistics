We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.57\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.57)(0.43)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0502673 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (The common notation is \(\hat{p}\) for a specific proportion.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.5412-0.57}{0.0502673} \\\\ &= -0.5729368 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < -0.5729368) ~=~ 0.2833438 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < -0.58) ~=~ 0.281 \] \[P(Z < -0.57) ~=~ 0.2843 \]
