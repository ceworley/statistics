We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (69)(91.7) ~=~ 6327.3 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (5.28)\sqrt{69} = 43.858974\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 6327.3 and a standard deviation of 43.858974. \[\sum{X} \sim N(6327.3,\,43.858974) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{12.7}{43.858974} = 0.2895645 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.28\) or \(z=0.29\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| < 0.2895645) = 0.2278505 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.28) = 0.2206 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.29) = 0.2282 \]
