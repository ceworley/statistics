We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (146)(88.52) ~=~ 12923.92 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (3.49)\sqrt{146} = 42.1698304\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 12923.92 and a standard deviation of 42.1698304. \[\sum{X} \sim N(12923.92,\,42.1698304) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{76.08}{42.1698304} = 1.8041334 \]

You can round that to \(z=1.8\) or \(z=1.81\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| > 1.8041334) = 0.0712104 \] \[P(|Z| > 1.8) = 0.0718 \] \[P(|Z| > 1.81) = 0.0702 \]
