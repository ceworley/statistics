We calculate the mean and standard deviation of the random sum using the formulas from the central limit theorem. \[\begin{aligned} \mu_{ \sum X} &= n\mu_{ X} \\ &= (144)(5.5) \\ &= 792 \end{aligned}\]

\[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{ \sum X} &= \sigma_{ X} \sqrt{n} \\ &= (2.8722813)\sqrt{144} \\ &= 34.4673759 \end{aligned}\]

The central limit theorem tells us that the random sum is approximately normal with the parameters calculated above.

\[\sum X \sim N(792,\,34.4673759) \]

In other words, we can approximate the summing of 144 rolls of 10-sided dice with a single spin of the following spinner.


Find the appropriate \(z\) score.

\[z ~=~ \frac{(\sum x)-\mu_{ \sum X}}{\sigma_{ \sum X}} ~=~ \frac{821.5-792}{34.4673759} ~=~ 0.8558818\]

You can round this to either 0.85 or 0.86. I will continue with the unrounded \(z\) score. We now rephrase the question as a standard normal probability.

\[P\left(\sum X < 821.5\right) ~=~ P(Z<0.8558818) \] \[P(Z<0.8558818) ~=~ \, ?\]
