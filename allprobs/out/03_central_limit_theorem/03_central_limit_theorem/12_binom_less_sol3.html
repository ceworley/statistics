We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (107)(0.31) ~=~ 33.17\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(107)(0.31)(0.69)} ~=~ 4.7840673 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{38.5-33.17}{4.7840673} \\\\ &= 1.1141148 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < 1.1141148) ~=~ 0.867385 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < 1.11) ~=~ 0.8665 \] \[P(Z < 1.12) ~=~ 0.8686 \]
