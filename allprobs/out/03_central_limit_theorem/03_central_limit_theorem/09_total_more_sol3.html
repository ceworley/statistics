We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (154)(74.25) ~=~ 11434.5 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (7.11)\sqrt{154} = 88.2327796\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 11434.5 and a standard deviation of 88.2327796. \[\sum{X} \sim N(11434.5,\,88.2327796) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{11400-11434.5}{88.2327796} = -0.3910111 \]

You can round that to \(z=-0.4\) or \(z=-0.39\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z > -0.3910111) = 0.6521055 \] \[P(Z > -0.4) = 0.6554 \] \[P(Z > -0.39) = 0.6517 \]
