We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.45\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.45)(0.55)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0552771 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (The common notation is \(\hat{p}\) for a specific proportion.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.4383-0.45}{0.0552771} \\\\ &= -0.211661 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < -0.211661) ~=~ 0.4161858 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < -0.22) ~=~ 0.4129 \] \[P(Z < -0.21) ~=~ 0.4168 \]
