We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.14\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.14)(0.86)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0270129 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (The common notation is \(\hat{p}\) for a specific proportion.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.1182-0.14}{0.0270129} \\\\ &= -0.8070217 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < -0.8070217) ~=~ 0.209827 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < -0.81) ~=~ 0.209 \] \[P(Z < -0.8) ~=~ 0.2119 \]
