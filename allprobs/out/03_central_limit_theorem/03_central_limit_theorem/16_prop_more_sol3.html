We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.45\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.45)(0.55)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0397043 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (The common notation is \(\hat{p}\) for a specific proportion.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.4427-0.45}{0.0397043} \\\\ &= -0.1838591 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z > -0.1838591) ~=~ 0.572938 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z > -0.19) ~=~ 0.5753 \] \[P(Z > -0.18) ~=~ 0.5714 \]
