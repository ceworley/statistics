We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 72.39 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{9.58}{\sqrt{802}} = 0.3382816\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 72.39 and a standard deviation of 0.3382816. \[\bar{X} \sim N(72.39,\,0.3382816) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{72.68-72.39}{0.3382816} = 0.857274 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.85\) or \(z=0.86\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z > 0.857274) = 0.1956467 \] \[P(Z > 0.85) = 0.1977 \] \[P(Z > 0.86) = 0.1949 \]
