We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 43.75 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{3.01}{\sqrt{532}} = 0.1305001\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 43.75 and a standard deviation of 0.1305001. \[\bar{X} \sim N(43.75,\,0.1305001) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{43.69-43.75}{0.1305001} = -0.4597699 \]

You can round that to \(z=-0.46\) or \(z=-0.45\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z > -0.4597699) = 0.6771593 \] \[P(Z > -0.46) = 0.6772 \] \[P(Z > -0.45) = 0.6736 \]
