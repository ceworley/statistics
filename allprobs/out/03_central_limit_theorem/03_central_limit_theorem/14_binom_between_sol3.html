We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (98)(0.48) ~=~ 47.04\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(98)(0.48)(0.52)} ~=~ 4.9457861 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{x_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{40.5-47.04}{4.9457861} \\\\ &= -1.3223378 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{x_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{46.5-47.04}{4.9457861} \\\\ &= -0.1091839 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(-1.3223378 < Z < -0.1091839) ~=~ 0.3635005 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(-1.33 < Z < -0.11) ~=~ 0.3644 \] \[P(-1.32 < Z < -0.1) ~=~ 0.3668 \] \[P(-1.33 < Z < -0.1) ~=~ 0.3628 \] \[P(-1.32 < Z < -0.11) ~=~ 0.3684 \]
