We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.85\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.85)(0.15)}{122}} ~=~ 0.0323277 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.8238-0.85}{0.0323277} \\\\ &= -0.8113626 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{\hat{p}_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.8648-0.85}{0.0323277} \\\\ &= 0.4563914 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(-0.8113626 < Z < 0.4563914) ~=~ 0.467367 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(-0.82 < Z < 0.45) ~=~ 0.4675 \] \[P(-0.81 < Z < 0.46) ~=~ 0.4682 \] \[P(-0.82 < Z < 0.46) ~=~ 0.4646 \] \[P(-0.81 < Z < 0.45) ~=~ 0.4711 \]
