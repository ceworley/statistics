We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (139)(86.24) ~=~ 11987.36 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (8.63)\sqrt{139} = 101.7461994\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 11987.36 and a standard deviation of 101.7461994. \[\sum{X} \sim N(11987.36,\,101.7461994) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{12100-11987.36}{101.7461994} = 1.1070684 \]

You can round that to \(z=1.1\) or \(z=1.11\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z > 1.1070684) = 0.1341322 \] \[P(Z > 1.1) = 0.1357 \] \[P(Z > 1.11) = 0.1335 \]
