First, determine \(z^\star\) such that \(P(|Z|<z^\star)=0.5\). It can help to draw a sketch.


The entire area under the density curve is 1. Thus, we can determine the areas of the tails (using symmetry). \[\text{area of each tail} = \frac{1-\gamma}{2} = \frac{1-0.5}{2} = 0.25\]


Determine a leftward area with boundary \(z^\star\). \[P(Z<z^\star)=\gamma + \frac{1-\gamma}{2}= 0.5+0.25=0.75\]


We rephrase the puzzle. We wish to determine \(z^\star\) such that \(P(Z<z^\star)=0.75\). This is easy to determine using a \(z\)-table, qnorm in R, norm.inv in spreadsheets, or other methods for evaluating the quantile function.

## Using R's qnorm:
qnorm(0.75)
## [1] 0.6744898

\[P(Z<0.67)=0.75\] In the original phrasing,

\[P(|Z|<0.67)=0.5\]

So, \(z^\star = 0.67\). Now, use the given expressions to evaluate the boundaries.


  1. Lower bound = \(~0.1716-0.67\sqrt{\frac{(0.1716)(1-0.1716)}{169}} ~=~ 0.15\)
  2. Upper bound = \(~0.1716+0.67\sqrt{\frac{(0.1716)(1-0.1716)}{169}} ~=~ 0.19\)
