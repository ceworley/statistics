First, determine \(z^\star\) such that \(P(|Z|<z^\star)=0.73\). It can help to draw a sketch.


The entire area under the density curve is 1. Thus, we can determine the areas of the tails (using symmetry). \[\text{area of each tail} = \frac{1-\gamma}{2} = \frac{1-0.73}{2} = 0.135\]


Determine a leftward area with boundary \(z^\star\). \[P(Z<z^\star)=\gamma + \frac{1-\gamma}{2}= 0.73+0.135=0.865\]


We rephrase the puzzle. We wish to determine \(z^\star\) such that \(P(Z<z^\star)=0.865\). This is easy to determine using a \(z\)-table, qnorm in R, norm.inv in spreadsheets, or other methods for evaluating the quantile function.

## Using R's qnorm:
qnorm(0.865)
