x = c(788, 735, 714, 776, 794, 760, 831, 774, 786, 810, 764, 783, 798, 803, 862, 737, 797, 882, 848, 764, 759)
n = length(x)
xbar = mean(x)
s = sd(x)
SE = s/sqrt(n)
gamma = 0.8   # Probability T is between -tstar and tstar
cumulative = gamma+(1-gamma)/2   # Probability T is less than tstar
tstar = qt(cumulative, n-1)
ME = tstar*s/sqrt(n)
LB = xbar-ME
UB = xbar+ME
print(data.frame(n,xbar,s,SE,tstar,ME,LB,UB,row.names=""))

  1. The sample size \(=n=21\).
  2. The sample mean \(=\bar{x}=\frac{\sum x}{n}=788.81\).
  3. The sample standard deviation \(=s=\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})^2}{n-1}}=41.66\).
  4. The estimated standard error \(=\text{SE}=\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{41.66}{\sqrt{21}}=9.09\).
  5. You can show, when \(n=21\), then \(\text{df}=20\), and \(P(|T|<1.33)=0.8\), so the boundary \(=t^\star=1.33\).
  6. The estimated margin of error \(=\text{ME}=\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{(1.33)(41.66)}{\sqrt{21}}=12.05\).
  7. The lower boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{LB}=\bar{x}-\text{ME}=788.81-12.05=776.76\).
  8. The upper boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{UB}=\bar{x}+\text{ME}=788.81+12.05=800.86\).
