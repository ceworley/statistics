The first 4 questions involve adding up relative frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=0.02667 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 2 measurements less than 36.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<36.5]=\frac{2}{75}=0.02667\).
  2. (ANS=0.9733 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 73 measurements more than 36.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>36.5]=\frac{73}{75}=0.9733\).
  3. (ANS=0.12 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 9 measurements closer than 0.75 units from 36.75. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-36.75|<0.75]=0.12\).
  4. (ANS=0.8933 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 67 measurements further than 0.5 units from 37. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-37|>0.5]=0.8933\).
  5. (ANS=37.5) First, \(0.1333 \cdot 75 = 10\). A boundary at \(b=37.5\) has 10 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<37.5]=0.1333\).
  6. (ANS=37) First, \(0.9467 \cdot 75 = 71\). A boundary at \(b=37\) has 71 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>37]=0.9467\).
  7. (ANS=1.5) First, \(0.3067 \cdot 75 = 23\). An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 37.5 contains 23 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-37.5|<1.5]=0.3067\).
