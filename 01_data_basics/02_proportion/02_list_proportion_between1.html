01_data_basics/02_proportion/02_list_proportion_between_v1

The lengths (in centimeters) of 10 lizards were recorded.

\[\mathbf{x} = 88, 85, 87, 82, 81, 85, 84, 83, 88, 83 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): lizard_data.csv


  1. What proportion of the measurements are inside 85.5 cm to 88.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>85.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<88.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[85.5<\mathbf{x}<88.5]\).
  2. What proportion of the measurements are outside 84.5 cm to 87.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<84.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>87.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(84.5<\mathbf{x}<87.5)]\).
  3. What proportion of the measurements are closer than 3.5 cm from 85 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-85\big|<3.5\right]\).
  4. What proportion of the measurements are farther than 1.5 cm from 84 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-84\big|>1.5\right]\).
  5. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 85 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-85\big|<r\right] ~=~ 0.9\).
  6. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 81 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-81\big|>r\right] ~=~ 0.9\).
