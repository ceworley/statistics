  1. (ANS=0.2) The proportion of measurements between 33.5 and 36.5 is 0.2. \[\text{prop}[33.5<\mathbf{x}<36.5]=0.2\]
  2. (ANS=0.9) The proportion of measurements outside 33.5 and 35.5 is 0.9. \[\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(33.5<\mathbf{x}<35.5)]=0.9\]
  3. (ANS=0.3) The proportion of measurements closer than 1 cm from 32.5 cm is 0.3. \[\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-32.5\big|<1\right] = 0.3\]
  4. (ANS=0.7) The proportion of measurements farther than 1 cm from 36.5 cm is 0.7. \[\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-36.5\big|>1\right] = 0.7\]
  5. (ANS=3.5) You can guess and check until you find a half-integer that satisfies the equation. It turns out that \(r = 3.5\) satisfies the equation, because \[\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-36\big|<3.5\right] ~=~ 0.8\]
  6. (ANS=0.5) You can guess and check until you find a half-integer that satisfies the equation. It turns out that \(r = 0.5\) satisfies the equation, because \[\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-38\big|>0.5\right] ~=~ 0.9\]
