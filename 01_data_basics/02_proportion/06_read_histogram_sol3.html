You may find it helpful to convert the densities to frequencies by multiplying each density by both the total sample size (\(n=50\)) and the width of the bar (2).


The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=0.68) There are 34 measurements less than 64. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<64]=0.68\).
  2. (ANS=0.72) There are 36 measurements more than 56. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>56]=0.72\).
  3. (ANS=0.08) There are 4 measurements closer than 1 units from 59. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-59|<1]=0.08\).
  4. (ANS=0.82) There are 41 measurements further than 2 units from 58. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-58|>2]=0.82\).
  5. (ANS=62) A boundary at \(b=62\) has 31 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<62]=0.62\).
  6. (ANS=60) A boundary at \(b=60\) has 27 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>60]=0.54\).
  7. (ANS=4) An interval with radius \(r=4\) around center 60 contains 20 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-60|<4]=0.4\).
  8. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 59 excludes 46 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-59|>1]=0.92\).

ANSWERS: 0.68, 0.72, 0.08, 0.82, 62, 60, 4, 1
