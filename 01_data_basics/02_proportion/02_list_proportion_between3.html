01_data_basics/02_proportion/02_list_proportion_between_v3

The lengths (in centimeters) of 10 lizards were recorded.

\[\mathbf{x} = 25, 25, 24, 21, 28, 28, 22, 23, 21, 27 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): lizard_data.csv


  1. What proportion of the measurements are inside 26.5 cm to 29.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>26.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<29.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[26.5<\mathbf{x}<29.5]\).
  2. What proportion of the measurements are outside 23.5 cm to 25.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<23.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>25.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(23.5<\mathbf{x}<25.5)]\).
  3. What proportion of the measurements are closer than 1.5 cm from 25 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-25\big|<1.5\right]\).
  4. What proportion of the measurements are farther than 0.5 cm from 29 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-29\big|>0.5\right]\).
  5. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 27 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-27\big|<r\right] ~=~ 0.1\).
  6. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 24 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-24\big|>r\right] ~=~ 0.2\).
