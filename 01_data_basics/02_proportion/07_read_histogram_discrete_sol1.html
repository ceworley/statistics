The first 4 questions involve adding up relative frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.

You may also find it is helpful to determine frequencies (counts). You do this by multiplying each relative frequency by the total number of measurements.



  1. (ANS=0.85) There are 17 measurements less than 4.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<4.5]=\frac{17}{20}=0.85\).
  2. (ANS=0.7) There are 14 measurements more than 1.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>1.5]=\frac{14}{20}=0.7\).
  3. (ANS=0.1) There are 2 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 5. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-5|<0.5]=\frac{2}{20}=0.1\).
  4. (ANS=0.7) There are 14 measurements further than 1 units from 3.5. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|>1]=\frac{14}{20}=0.7\).
  5. (ANS=5.5) First, convert the proportion into a count. \(0.95\cdot20=19\). A boundary at \(b=5.5\) has 19 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<5.5]=0.95\).
  6. (ANS=3.5) First, convert the proportion into a count. \(0.35\cdot20=7\). A boundary at \(b=3.5\) has 7 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>3.5]=0.35\).
  7. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 2 contains 5 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-2|<0.5]=0.25\).
  8. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 3.5 excludes 14 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|>1]=0.7\).
