The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=69) There are 69 measurements less than 29.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<29.5]=69\).
  2. (ANS=104) There are 104 measurements more than 28.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>28.5]=104\).
  3. (ANS=6) There are 6 measurements closer than 0.25 units from 28.25. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-28.25|<0.25]=6\).
  4. (ANS=69) There are 69 measurements further than 1.25 units from 28.25. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-28.25|>1.25]=69\).
  5. (ANS=28.5) A boundary at \(b=28.5\) has 26 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<28.5]=26\).
  6. (ANS=29) A boundary at \(b=29\) has 91 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>29]=91\).
  7. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 28 contains 14 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-28|<0.5]=14\).
  8. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 29 excludes 87 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-29|>0.5]=87\).
