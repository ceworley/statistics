The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=86) There are 86 measurements less than 5.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<5.5]=86\).
  2. (ANS=48) There are 48 measurements more than 3.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>3.5]=48\).
  3. (ANS=55) There are 55 measurements closer than 1.5 units from 4. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4|<1.5]=55\).
  4. (ANS=63) There are 63 measurements further than 1 units from 2.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-2.5|>1]=63\).
  5. (ANS=2.5) A boundary at \(b=2.5\) has 31 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<2.5]=31\).
  6. (ANS=2.5) A boundary at \(b=2.5\) has 69 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>2.5]=69\).
  7. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 3 contains 55 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|<1.5]=55\).
  8. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 3 excludes 79 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|>0.5]=79\).
