The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=81) There are 81 measurements less than 5.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<5.5]=81\).
  2. (ANS=49) There are 49 measurements more than 3.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>3.5]=49\).
  3. (ANS=14) There are 14 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 3. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|<0.5]=14\).
  4. (ANS=36) There are 36 measurements further than 2 units from 3.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|>2]=36\).
  5. (ANS=4.5) A boundary at \(b=4.5\) has 62 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<4.5]=62\).
  6. (ANS=3.5) A boundary at \(b=3.5\) has 49 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>3.5]=49\).
  7. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 3.5 contains 25 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|<1]=25\).
  8. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 3 excludes 55 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|>1.5]=55\).
