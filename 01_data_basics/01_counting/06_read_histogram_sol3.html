The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=79) There are 79 measurements less than 68. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<68]=79\).
  2. (ANS=116) There are 116 measurements more than 58. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>58]=116\).
  3. (ANS=10) There are 10 measurements closer than 1 units from 63. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-63|<1]=10\).
  4. (ANS=112) There are 112 measurements further than 2 units from 58. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-58|>2]=112\).
  5. (ANS=68) A boundary at \(b=68\) has 79 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<68]=79\).
  6. (ANS=56) A boundary at \(b=56\) has 126 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>56]=126\).
  7. (ANS=2) An interval with radius \(r=2\) around center 60 contains 17 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-60|<2]=17\).
  8. (ANS=6) An interval with radius \(r=6\) around center 62 excludes 55 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-62|>6]=55\).
