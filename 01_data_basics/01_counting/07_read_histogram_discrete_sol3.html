The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=43) There are 43 measurements less than 5.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<5.5]=43\).
  2. (ANS=70) There are 70 measurements more than 1.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>1.5]=70\).
  3. (ANS=24) There are 24 measurements closer than 1.5 units from 5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-5|<1.5]=24\).
  4. (ANS=47) There are 47 measurements further than 2 units from 3.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|>2]=47\).
  5. (ANS=9.5) A boundary at \(b=9.5\) has 75 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<9.5]=75\).
  6. (ANS=7.5) A boundary at \(b=7.5\) has 22 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>7.5]=22\).
  7. (ANS=3) An interval with radius \(r=3\) around center 6.5 contains 46 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-6.5|<3]=46\).
  8. (ANS=2.5) An interval with radius \(r=2.5\) around center 5 excludes 42 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-5|>2.5]=42\).
