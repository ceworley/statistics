01_data_basics/01_counting/05_read_frequency_distribution_v2

A random procedure generated 90 measurements, which were organized into the frequency distribution shown below. You can assume the measurements are of a continuous random variable, such that every measurement is in one of the intervals (and not on a break).

interval frequency
65 — 70 28
70 — 75 7
75 — 80 3
80 — 85 10
85 — 90 42

  1. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}<80]\).
  2. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}>70]\).
  3. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-72.5|<2.5]\).
  4. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-77.5|>7.5]\).
  5. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}<b] = 35\).
  6. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}>b] = 52\).
  7. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-77.5|<r] = 3\).
  8. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-75|>r] = 80\).
