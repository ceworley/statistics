The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=25) There are 25 measurements less than 3.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<3.5]=25\).
  2. (ANS=32) There are 32 measurements more than 4.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>4.5]=32\).
  3. (ANS=11) There are 11 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 3. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|<0.5]=11\).
  4. (ANS=41) There are 41 measurements further than 1.5 units from 5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-5|>1.5]=41\).
  5. (ANS=4.5) A boundary at \(b=4.5\) has 38 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<4.5]=38\).
  6. (ANS=4.5) A boundary at \(b=4.5\) has 32 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>4.5]=32\).
  7. (ANS=2.5) An interval with radius \(r=2.5\) around center 4 contains 45 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4|<2.5]=45\).
  8. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 3 excludes 59 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|>0.5]=59\).
