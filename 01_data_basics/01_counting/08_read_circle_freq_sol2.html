The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=68) There are 68 measurements less than 5.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<5.5]=68\).
  2. (ANS=23) There are 23 measurements more than 6.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>6.5]=23\).
  3. (ANS=30) There are 30 measurements closer than 1 units from 2.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-2.5|<1]=30\).
  4. (ANS=87) There are 87 measurements further than 0.5 units from 5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-5|>0.5]=87\).
  5. (ANS=7.5) A boundary at \(b=7.5\) has 87 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<7.5]=87\).
  6. (ANS=2.5) A boundary at \(b=2.5\) has 71 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>2.5]=71\).
  7. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 4 contains 39 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4|<1.5]=39\).
  8. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 5 excludes 69 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-5|>1.5]=69\).
