01_data_basics/01_counting/05_read_frequency_distribution_v3

A random procedure generated 125 measurements, which were organized into the frequency distribution shown below. You can assume the measurements are of a continuous random variable, such that every measurement is in one of the intervals (and not on a break).

interval frequency
70 — 75 30
75 — 80 29
80 — 85 37
85 — 90 29

  1. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}<85]\).
  2. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}>80]\).
  3. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-82.5|<2.5]\).
  4. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-80|>5]\).
  5. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}<b] = 96\).
  6. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}>b] = 66\).
  7. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-77.5|<r] = 29\).
  8. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-80|>r] = 59\).
