The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=7) There are 7 measurements less than 2.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<2.5]=7\).
  2. (ANS=12) There are 12 measurements more than 9.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>9.5]=12\).
  3. (ANS=10) There are 10 measurements closer than 2 units from 3.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|<2]=10\).
  4. (ANS=31) There are 31 measurements further than 1 units from 9.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-9.5|>1]=31\).
  5. (ANS=9.5) A boundary at \(b=9.5\) has 28 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<9.5]=28\).
  6. (ANS=4.5) A boundary at \(b=4.5\) has 29 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>4.5]=29\).
  7. (ANS=2.5) An interval with radius \(r=2.5\) around center 8 contains 17 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-8|<2.5]=17\).
  8. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 5 excludes 30 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-5|>1.5]=30\).
