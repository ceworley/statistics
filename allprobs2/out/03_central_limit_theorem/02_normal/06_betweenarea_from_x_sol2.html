    First, draw a sketch. We can label the CC axis by adding integer multiples of 5 to 91. We know to shade the between 8989 and 9191 because those values satisfy the condition 89<X<9189 < X < 91.


    We calculate the zz values of the boundaries. Left boundary: z1=x1−μσ=89−915=−0.4\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{x_1-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{89-91}{5} \\\\ &= -0.4 \end{aligned}

    Right boundary:

    z2=x2−μσ=91−915=0\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{x_2-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{91-91}{5} \\\\ &= 0 \end{aligned}

    We rephrase our problem into a standard normal probability problem:

    P(89<X<91)=P(−0.4<Z<0)P\left(89 < X < 91\right) ~=~ P(-0.4<Z<0)

    So, we just need to evaluate P(−0.4<Z<0)P(-0.4<Z<0).

    From here we have a formula that lets us use the zz table. (We practiced this part before.) P(z1<Z<z2)=P(Z<z2)−P(Z<z1)P(−0.4<Z<0)=P(Z<0)−P(Z<−0.4)=0.5−0.3446=0.1554\begin{aligned} P(z_1 < Z < z_2) &= P(Z<z_2) - P(Z<z1) \\ P(-0.4<Z<0) &= P(Z<0)-P(Z<-0.4)\\ &= 0.5-0.3446 \\ &= 0.1554 \end{aligned}
