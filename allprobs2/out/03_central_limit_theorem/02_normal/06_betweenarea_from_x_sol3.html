    First, draw a sketch. We can label the HH axis by adding integer multiples of 100 to 450. We know to shade the between 350350 and 540540 because those values satisfy the condition 350<X<540350 < X < 540.


    We calculate the zz values of the boundaries. Left boundary: z1=x1−μσ=350−450100=−1\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{x_1-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{350-450}{100} \\\\ &= -1 \end{aligned}

    Right boundary:

    z2=x2−μσ=540−450100=0.9\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{x_2-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{540-450}{100} \\\\ &= 0.9 \end{aligned}

    We rephrase our problem into a standard normal probability problem:

    P(350<X<540)=P(−1<Z<0.9)P\left(350 < X < 540\right) ~=~ P(-1<Z<0.9)

    So, we just need to evaluate P(−1<Z<0.9)P(-1<Z<0.9).

    From here we have a formula that lets us use the zz table. (We practiced this part before.) P(z1<Z<z2)=P(Z<z2)−P(Z<z1)P(−1<Z<0.9)=P(Z<0.9)−P(Z<−1)=0.8159−0.1587=0.6572\begin{aligned} P(z_1 < Z < z_2) &= P(Z<z_2) - P(Z<z1) \\ P(-1<Z<0.9) &= P(Z<0.9)-P(Z<-1)\\ &= 0.8159-0.1587 \\ &= 0.6572 \end{aligned}
