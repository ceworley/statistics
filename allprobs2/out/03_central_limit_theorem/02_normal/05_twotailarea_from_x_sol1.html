    First, draw a sketch. We can label the BB axis by adding integer multiples of 150 to 480. We know to shade the two tails because values far from 480 satisfy the condition |B−480|>60\big|B-480\big| > 60. We draw the boundaries at 480−60=420480-60=420 and 480+60=540480+60=540 because those are the solutions to |B−480|=60\big|B-480\big| = 60. We can also rephrase the probability.

    P(|B−480|>60)=P(B<420 OR B>540)P\left(\big|B-480\big| > 60\right) ~=~ P\big(B<420 \text{ OR } B>540\big)


    We calculate the zz values of the boundaries. Left boundary: z1=420−480150=−60150=−0.4\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{420-480}{150} \\\\ &= \frac{-60}{150} \\\\ &= -0.4 \end{aligned}

    Right boundary:

    z2=540−480150=60150=0.4\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{540-480}{150} \\\\ &= \frac{60}{150} \\\\ &= 0.4 \end{aligned}

    We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because

    P(|B−480|>60)=P(|Z|>0.4)P\left(\big|B-480\big| > 60\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big|>0.4\right)

    So, we just need to evaluate P(|Z|>0.4)P(|Z|>0.4). I will also point out that 60150=0.4\frac{60}{150} = 0.4. In general, if XX is normally distributed, then: P(|X−μ|>d)=P(|Z|>dσ)P\left(\big|X-\mu\big|>d\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big| > \frac{d}{\sigma}\right)

    From here we have a formula that lets us use the zz table. (We practiced this part before.) P(|Z|>0.4)=2−2×P(Z<0.4)=2−2×0.6554=0.6892\begin{aligned} P(|Z|>0.4) &= 2- 2 \times P(Z<0.4) \\ &= 2 - 2 \times 0.6554 \\ &= 0.6892 \end{aligned}
