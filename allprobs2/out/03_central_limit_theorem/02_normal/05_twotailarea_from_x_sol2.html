    First, draw a sketch. We can label the BB axis by adding integer multiples of 50 to 460. We know to shade the two tails because values far from 460 satisfy the condition |B−460|>70\big|B-460\big| > 70. We draw the boundaries at 460−70=390460-70=390 and 460+70=530460+70=530 because those are the solutions to |B−460|=70\big|B-460\big| = 70. We can also rephrase the probability.

    P(|B−460|>70)=P(B<390 OR B>530)P\left(\big|B-460\big| > 70\right) ~=~ P\big(B<390 \text{ OR } B>530\big)


    We calculate the zz values of the boundaries. Left boundary: z1=390−46050=−7050=−1.4\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{390-460}{50} \\\\ &= \frac{-70}{50} \\\\ &= -1.4 \end{aligned}

    Right boundary:

    z2=530−46050=7050=1.4\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{530-460}{50} \\\\ &= \frac{70}{50} \\\\ &= 1.4 \end{aligned}

    We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because

    P(|B−460|>70)=P(|Z|>1.4)P\left(\big|B-460\big| > 70\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big|>1.4\right)

    So, we just need to evaluate P(|Z|>1.4)P(|Z|>1.4). I will also point out that 7050=1.4\frac{70}{50} = 1.4. In general, if XX is normally distributed, then: P(|X−μ|>d)=P(|Z|>dσ)P\left(\big|X-\mu\big|>d\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big| > \frac{d}{\sigma}\right)

    From here we have a formula that lets us use the zz table. (We practiced this part before.) P(|Z|>1.4)=2−2×P(Z<1.4)=2−2×0.9192=0.1616\begin{aligned} P(|Z|>1.4) &= 2- 2 \times P(Z<1.4) \\ &= 2 - 2 \times 0.9192 \\ &= 0.1616 \end{aligned}
