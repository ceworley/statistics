First, draw a sketch. We can label the KK axis by adding integer multiples of 1 to 10. We know to shade the between 99 and 1010 because those values satisfy the condition 9<X<109 < X < 10.


We calculate the zz values of the boundaries. Left boundary: z1=x1−μσ=9−101=−1\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{x_1-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{9-10}{1} \\\\ &= -1 \end{aligned}

Right boundary:

z2=x2−μσ=10−101=0\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{x_2-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{10-10}{1} \\\\ &= 0 \end{aligned}

We rephrase our problem into a standard normal probability problem:

P(9<X<10)=P(−1<Z<0)P\left(9 < X < 10\right) ~=~ P(-1<Z<0)

So, we just need to evaluate P(−1<Z<0)P(-1<Z<0).

From here we have a formula that lets us use the zz table. (We practiced this part before.) P(z1<Z<z2)=P(Z<z2)−P(Z<z1)P(−1<Z<0)=P(Z<0)−P(Z<−1)=0.5−0.1587=0.3413\begin{aligned} P(z_1 < Z < z_2) &= P(Z<z_2) - P(Z<z1) \\ P(-1<Z<0) &= P(Z<0)-P(Z<-1)\\ &= 0.5-0.1587 \\ &= 0.3413 \end{aligned}
