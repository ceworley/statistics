    First, draw a sketch. We can label the BB axis by adding integer multiples of 150 to 480. We know to shade toward the left because small values of BB satisfy the condition B<390B<390.


    We are given a specific bb value as a boundary. (Remember, for random variables we use uppercase letters, but for specific values we use lowercase.) b=390b = 390 We calculate the zz value of the boundary. z=b−μσ=390−480150=−0.6\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{b-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{390-480}{150} \\\\ &= -0.6 \end{aligned}

    We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because P(B<390)=P(Z<−0.6)P(B<390) ~=~ P(Z<-0.6)

    So, we just need to evaluate P(Z<−0.6)P(Z<-0.6). To do this, you just need a zz-table.
