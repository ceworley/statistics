    First, draw a sketch. We can label the MM axis by adding integer multiples of 40 to 270. We know to shade toward the left because small values of MM satisfy the condition M<320M<320.


    We are given a specific mm value as a boundary. (Remember, for random variables we use uppercase letters, but for specific values we use lowercase.) m=320m = 320 We calculate the zz value of the boundary. z=m−μσ=320−27040=1.25\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{m-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{320-270}{40} \\\\ &= 1.25 \end{aligned}

    We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because P(M<320)=P(Z<1.25)P(M<320) ~=~ P(Z<1.25)
