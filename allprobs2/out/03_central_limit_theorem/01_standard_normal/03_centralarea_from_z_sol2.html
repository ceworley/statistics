1. solution_03_central_limit_theorem/01_standard_normal/03_centralarea_from_z

    First, you need to identify that we are looking for a central area. This is because zz-scores near 0 satisfy |Z|<0.56|Z|<0.56, while zz-scores far from 0 (either positive or negative) do not satisfy the inequality.

    Start with a sketch.


    Remember, the entire area is always 1. Most z-tables only provide left areas, so three methods are shown to get the central area from a left-area (cumulative probability) table.

    Method 1: First find the area of the left tail. P(Z<−0.56)=0.2877P(Z<-0.56) = 0.2877 Recognize normal distributions are symmetric, so we also know the area of the right tail. P(Z>0.56)=0.2877P(Z>0.56) = 0.2877 The three areas add to 1. P(|Z|<0.56)=1−P(Z<−0.56)−P(Z>0.56)=1−0.2877−0.2877=0.4246\begin{aligned} P\left(|Z|<0.56\right) &= 1-P(Z<-0.56)-P(Z>0.56) \\ &= 1 - 0.2877 - 0.2877 \\ &= 0.4246 \end{aligned}

    This technique can be summarized with the following formula: P(|Z|<z)=1−2⋅P(Z<−z)P\left(|Z|<z\right) = 1-2\cdot P(Z<-z) assuming z>0z>0.

    So, in our case (when z=0.56z=0.56), P(|Z|<0.56)=1−2⋅P(Z<−0.56)=1−2⋅0.2877=0.4246\begin{aligned} P\left(|Z|<0.56\right) &= 1-2\cdot P(Z<-0.56) \\ &= 1-2\cdot0.2877 \\ &= 0.4246 \end{aligned}

    This method is shown graphically:


    Method 2: You can also find half of the central area and then double it. P(|Z|<z)=2⋅[P(Z<z)−0.5]=2⋅[0.7123−0.5]=2⋅[0.2123]=0.4246\begin{aligned} P\left(|Z|<z\right) &= 2\cdot\left[ P(Z<z)-0.5\right] \\ &= 2\cdot\left[ 0.7123-0.5\right] \\ &= 2\cdot\left[ 0.2123\right] \\ &= 0.4246 \end{aligned}

    This method is shown graphically:


    Method 3: You can also calculate the central area with a difference of two left areas.

    P(|Z|<0.56)=P(Z<0.56)−P(Z<−0.56)=0.7123−0.2877=0.4246\begin{aligned} P\left(|Z|<0.56\right) &= P(Z<0.56)-P(Z<-0.56)\\ &= 0.7123-0.2877 \\ &= 0.4246 \end{aligned}

    In a spreadsheet, you could use the NORM.DIST() function.

    =NORM.DIST(0.56,0,1,TRUE) - NORM.DIST(-0.56,0,1,TRUE)

    In R, you could use the pnorm function.

    pnorm(0.56) - pnorm(-0.56)
