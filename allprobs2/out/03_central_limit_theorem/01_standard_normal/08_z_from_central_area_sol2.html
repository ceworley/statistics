    Start with a sketch.


    Method 1: Determine the area of each tail. Both tails have the same area, and all three areas add to 1. Thus, P(Z>z)=1−0.342=0.33P(Z>z) = \frac{1-0.34}{2} = 0.33

    P(Z<z)=0.33+0.34=0.67\begin{aligned} P(Z<z) &= 0.33+0.34 \\ &= 0.67 \end{aligned}

    You should go to your zz-table and find the zz-score with the left area closest to 0.67.

    zz P(Z<z)P(Z<z)
    0.41 0.6591
    0.42 0.6628
    0.43 0.6664
    0.44 0.67
    0.45 0.6736
    0.46 0.6772

    It turns out the exact answer is z=0.4399132z=0.4399132, which could be found by using an inverse normal function. On a spreadsheet:

    =Norm.Inv(0.67,0,1)

    Using R:

    centralarea = 0.34
leftarea = (1-centralarea)/2 + centralarea
qnorm( leftarea )

    But, because we are using the zz-table, I will accept either 0.43 or 0.44. (Or, really anything within 0.01 of 0.4399132.)

    Method 2: Another way to get 0.67 is by adding half of 0.34 to 0.5. P(Z<z)=0.342+0.5=0.17+0.5=0.67P(Z<z) ~=~ \frac{0.34}{2}+0.5~=~ 0.17+0.5 ~=~ 0.67


    Then, use the table. Or, you could use R:

    centralarea = 0.34
leftarea = 0.5 + centralarea/2
qnorm( leftarea )
