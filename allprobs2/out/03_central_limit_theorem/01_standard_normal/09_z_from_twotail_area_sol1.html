1. solution_03_central_limit_theorem/01_standard_normal/09_z_from_twotail_area

    Start with a sketch. The total two-tail area is 0.6, so each tail has half that area.


    Method 1: Determine the area of each tail and the center. Both tails have the same area, and all three areas add to 1. Thus, P(Z>z)=0.62=0.3P(Z>z) = \frac{0.6}{2} = 0.3

    P(Z<−z)=0.62=0.3P(Z<-z) = \frac{0.6}{2} = 0.3

    P(|Z|<z)=1−P(|Z|>z)=1−0.6=0.4\begin{aligned} P(|Z|<z) &= 1-P(|Z|>z) \\ &= 1-0.6 \\ &= 0.4 \end{aligned}

    Find the left area.

    P(Z<z)=0.3+0.4=0.7\begin{aligned} P(Z<z) &= 0.3+0.4 \\ &= 0.7 \end{aligned}
