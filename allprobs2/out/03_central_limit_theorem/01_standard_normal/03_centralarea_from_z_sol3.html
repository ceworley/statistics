First, you need to identify that we are looking for a central area. This is because zz-scores near 0 satisfy |Z|<0.54|Z|<0.54, while zz-scores far from 0 (either positive or negative) do not satisfy the inequality.

Start with a sketch.


Remember, the entire area is always 1. Most z-tables only provide left areas, so three methods are shown to get the central area from a left-area (cumulative probability) table.

Method 1: First find the area of the left tail. P(Z<−0.54)=0.2946P(Z<-0.54) = 0.2946 Recognize normal distributions are symmetric, so we also know the area of the right tail. P(Z>0.54)=0.2946P(Z>0.54) = 0.2946 The three areas add to 1. P(|Z|<0.54)=1−P(Z<−0.54)−P(Z>0.54)=1−0.2946−0.2946=0.4108\begin{aligned} P\left(|Z|<0.54\right) &= 1-P(Z<-0.54)-P(Z>0.54) \\ &= 1 - 0.2946 - 0.2946 \\ &= 0.4108 \end{aligned}

This technique can be summarized with the following formula: P(|Z|<z)=1−2⋅P(Z<−z)P\left(|Z|<z\right) = 1-2\cdot P(Z<-z) assuming z>0z>0.

So, in our case (when z=0.54z=0.54), P(|Z|<0.54)=1−2⋅P(Z<−0.54)=1−2⋅0.2946=0.4108\begin{aligned} P\left(|Z|<0.54\right) &= 1-2\cdot P(Z<-0.54) \\ &= 1-2\cdot0.2946 \\ &= 0.4108 \end{aligned}

Method 2: You can also find half of the central area and then double it. P(|Z|<z)=2⋅[P(Z<z)−0.5]=2⋅[0.7054−0.5]=2⋅[0.2054]=0.4108\begin{aligned} P\left(|Z|<z\right) &= 2\cdot\left[ P(Z<z)-0.5\right] \\ &= 2\cdot\left[ 0.7054-0.5\right] \\ &= 2\cdot\left[ 0.2054\right] \\ &= 0.4108 \end{aligned}

This method is shown graphically:


Method 3: You can also calculate the central area with a difference of two left areas.

P(|Z|<0.54)=P(Z<0.54)−P(Z<−0.54)=0.7054−0.2946=0.4108\begin{aligned} P\left(|Z|<0.54\right) &= P(Z<0.54)-P(Z<-0.54)\\ &= 0.7054-0.2946 \\ &= 0.4108 \end{aligned}
