  1. Solution

    Start with a sketch.


    Method 1: Determine the area of each tail. Both tails have the same area, and all three areas add to 1. Thus, P(Z>z)=1−0.322=0.34P(Z>z) = \frac{1-0.32}{2} = 0.34

    P(Z<z)=0.34+0.32=0.66\begin{aligned} P(Z<z) &= 0.34+0.32 \\ &= 0.66 \end{aligned}

    You should go to your zz-table and find the zz-score with the left area closest to 0.66.

    zz P(Z<z)P(Z<z)
    0.39 0.6517
    0.4 0.6554
    0.41 0.6591
    0.42 0.6628
    0.43 0.6664
    0.44 0.67

    It turns out the exact answer is z=0.4124631z=0.4124631, which could be found by using an inverse normal function. On a spreadsheet:

    =Norm.Inv(0.66,0,1)

    Using R:

    centralarea = 0.32
leftarea = (1-centralarea)/2 + centralarea
qnorm( leftarea )

    But, because we are using the zz-table, I will accept either 0.41 or 0.42. (Or, really anything within 0.01 of 0.4124631.)

    Method 2: Another way to get 0.66 is by adding half of 0.32 to 0.5. P(Z<z)=0.322+0.5=0.16+0.5=0.66P(Z<z) ~=~ \frac{0.32}{2}+0.5~=~ 0.16+0.5 ~=~ 0.66


    Then, use the table. Or, you could use R:

    centralarea = 0.32
leftarea = 0.5 + centralarea/2
qnorm( leftarea )
