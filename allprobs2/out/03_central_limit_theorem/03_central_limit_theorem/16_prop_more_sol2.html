  1. Solution

    We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. μ=p=0.14\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.14 σ=p(1−p)n=(0.14)(0.86)n=0.0270129\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.14)(0.86)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0270129

    We determine a zz-score. (The common notation is p̂\hat{p} for a specific proportion.) z=p̂−μσ=0.1182−0.140.0270129=−0.8070217\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.1182-0.14}{0.0270129} \\\\ &= -0.8070217 \end{aligned}

    Then, find the standard normal probability.

    P(Z>−0.8070217)=0.790173P(Z > -0.8070217) ~=~ 0.790173
