    We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. μ=np=(196)(0.9)=176.4\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (196)(0.9) ~=~ 176.4 σ=np(1−p)=(196)(0.9)(0.1)=4.2\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(196)(0.9)(0.1)} ~=~ 4.2

    We determine both zz-scores. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used ∑x\sum x to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use xx as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) We get the first zz-score.

    z1=x1−μσ=173.5−176.44.2=−0.6904762\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{x_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{173.5-176.4}{4.2} \\\\ &= -0.6904762 \end{aligned}

    We get the second zz-score.

    z2=x2−μσ=175.5−176.44.2=−0.2142857\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{x_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{175.5-176.4}{4.2} \\\\ &= -0.2142857 \end{aligned}

    Then, find the standard normal probability.

    P(−0.6904762<Z<−0.2142857)=0.1702147P(-0.6904762 < Z < -0.2142857) ~=~ 0.1702147
