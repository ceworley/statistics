    We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. μ=np=(165)(0.14)=23.1\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (165)(0.14) ~=~ 23.1 σ=np(1−p)=(165)(0.14)(0.86)=4.4571291\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(165)(0.14)(0.86)} ~=~ 4.4571291

    We determine a zz-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used ∑x\sum x to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use xx as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) z=x−μσ=19.5−23.14.4571291=−0.8076948\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{19.5-23.1}{4.4571291} \\\\ &= -0.8076948 \end{aligned}

    Then, find the standard normal probability.

    P(Z<−0.8076948)=0.2096331P(Z < -0.8076948) ~=~ 0.2096331
