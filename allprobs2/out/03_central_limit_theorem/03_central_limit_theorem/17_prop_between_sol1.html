  1. Solution

    We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. μ=p=0.9\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.9 σ=p(1−p)n=(0.9)(0.1)196=0.0214286\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.9)(0.1)}{196}} ~=~ 0.0214286

    We determine both zz-scores. We get the first zz-score.

    z1=p̂1−μσ=0.8852−0.90.0214286=−0.6904762\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.8852-0.9}{0.0214286} \\\\ &= -0.6904762 \end{aligned}

    We get the second zz-score.

    z2=p̂2−μσ=0.8954−0.90.0214286=−0.2142857\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{\hat{p}_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.8954-0.9}{0.0214286} \\\\ &= -0.2142857 \end{aligned}

    Then, find the standard normal probability.

    P(−0.6904762<Z<−0.2142857)=0.1702147P(-0.6904762 < Z < -0.2142857) ~=~ 0.1702147
