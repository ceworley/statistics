  1. Solution

    We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. μ=p=0.47\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.47 σ=p(1−p)n=(0.47)(0.53)122=0.0451863\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.47)(0.53)}{122}} ~=~ 0.0451863

    We determine both zz-scores. We get the first zz-score.

    z1=p̂1−μσ=0.4221−0.470.0451863=−1.0593661\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.4221-0.47}{0.0451863} \\\\ &= -1.0593661 \end{aligned}

    We get the second zz-score.

    z2=p̂2−μσ=0.5369−0.470.0451863=1.4802102\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{\hat{p}_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.5369-0.47}{0.0451863} \\\\ &= 1.4802102 \end{aligned}

    Then, find the standard normal probability.

    P(−1.0593661<Z<1.4802102)=0.7858749P(-1.0593661 < Z < 1.4802102) ~=~ 0.7858749
