    We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. μ=np=(97)(0.57)=55.29\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (97)(0.57) ~=~ 55.29 σ=np(1−p)=(97)(0.57)(0.43)=4.8759307\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(97)(0.57)(0.43)} ~=~ 4.8759307

    We determine a zz-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used ∑x\sum x to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use xx as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) z=x−μσ=52.5−55.294.8759307=−0.5721985\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{52.5-55.29}{4.8759307} \\\\ &= -0.5721985 \end{aligned}

    Then, find the standard normal probability.
