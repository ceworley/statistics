    This scenario describes a combinations problem (order does not matter). We are considering the subsets of size 4 from a set of size 22.

    nCr=n!(n−r)!⋅r!n=22r=422C4=22!(22−4)!⋅4!=22!18!⋅4!=22⋅21⋅20⋅194⋅3⋅2⋅1=7315\begin{aligned} {_nC_r} &= \frac{n!}{(n-r)! \cdot r!} \\\\ n &= 22 \\\\ r &= 4 \\\\ {_{22}C_{4}} &= \frac{22!}{(22-4)!\cdot 4! } \\\\ &= \frac{22!}{18! \cdot 4!} \\\\ &= \frac{ 22\cdot21\cdot20\cdot19}{ 4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1} \\\\ &= 7315 \end{aligned}
