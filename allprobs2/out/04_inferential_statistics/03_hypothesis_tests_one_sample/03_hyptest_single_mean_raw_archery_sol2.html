Determine the sample statistics (x‾\bar{x} and ss). I would recommend using a spreadsheet or R (or another computer-based method). x‾=−12.8\bar{x} = -12.8 s=31.04s = 31.04 Evaluate the tt statistic.

t=|−12.8−0|(31.04)/20=1.84t = \frac{|-12.8-0|}{(31.04)/\sqrt{20}} = 1.84 Restate the pp-value. p-value=P(|T|>1.84)p\text{-value} = P\left(|T|>1.84\right) Remember, the degree of freedom is one less than the sample size. df=19\text{df} = 19

At this point, there are various ways to determine the pp-value. The least accurate way is to use the tt-table. Go to the row with df=n−1=20−1=19\text{df}=n-1=20-1=19.


If we go to the row with df=19, we can see that our calculated tt, 1.84, is between 1.73 and 2.09. Thus, we know P(|T|>1.84)P(|T|>1.84) is between 0.05 and 0.1. You could get pretty close by using linear interpolation. P(|T|>1.84)≈(1.84−1.732.09−1.73)(0.05−0.1)+0.1=0.0847P(|T|>1.84)\approx \left(\frac{1.84-1.73}{2.09-1.73}\right)\left(0.05-0.1\right)+0.1 = 0.0847
