    We can approximate a 95% confidence interval of a proportion (where the 95% refers to how frequently these intervals straddle the population proportion) by using p̂±2p̂(1−p̂)n\hat{p} \pm \frac{2 \sqrt{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}}{\sqrt{n}}. lower boundary=p̂−2p̂(1−p̂)n\text{lower boundary} = \hat{p} - \frac{2 \sqrt{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}}{\sqrt{n}} upper boundary=p̂+2p̂(1−p̂)n\text{upper boundary} = \hat{p} + \frac{2 \sqrt{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}}{\sqrt{n}} where p̂\hat{p} is the sample proportion and nn is the sample size. The 2 comes from the fact that 95% of normal measurements land within 2 standard deviations from the mean.

    The quantity that we subtract from or add to the sample proportion is called the margin of error. margin of error=2p̂(1−p̂)n\text{margin of error} = \frac{2 \sqrt{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}}{\sqrt{n}}

    If we know p̂\hat{p} will be approximately 0.2, and we want the margin of error to be approximately 0.0032, then how large does the sample size need to be?
