  1. Question

    A sample was taken from a population. The measurements are shown below and can be downloaded as a csv.

    276, 369, 346, 316, 285, 278, 349, 283

    You are tasked with determining the confidence interval (of the population mean) with the confidence level of γ=0.9\gamma=0.9.


    1. Determine nn, the sample size.
    2. Determine x‾\bar{x}, the sample mean.
    3. Determine ss, the sample standard deviation (with Bessel correction).
    4. Calculate sn\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}, the estimated standard error.
    5. Determine t⋆t^\star such that P(|T|<t⋆)=0.9P(|T|<t^\star)=0.9. Remember, df=n−1\text{df}=n-1.
    6. Calculate t⋆sn\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}, the estimated margin of error.
    7. Calculate x‾−t⋆sn\bar{x}-\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}, the lower boundary of the 90% confidence interval.
    8. Calculate x‾+t⋆sn\bar{x}+\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}, the upper boundary of the 90% confidence interval.
