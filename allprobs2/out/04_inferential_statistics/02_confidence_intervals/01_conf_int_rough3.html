A scientist has weighed 179 specimens of a newly discovered organism. Those weights have a sample mean of x‾=25.1\bar{x} =25.1 grams and a sample standard deviation of s=7.6s=7.6 grams. The scientist hopes to construct a 95% confidence interval of the organism’s population mean (μ\mu).

The scientist will later consult a statistician for a more precise method, but for now she will use a quick method to estimate the 95% confidence interval: x‾±2sn\bar{x}\pm \frac{2s}{\sqrt{n}}

(You can round to nearest 0.1 grams.)


  1. Determine the lower boundary of the confidence interval by using x‾−2sn\bar{x}-\frac{2s}{\sqrt{n}}.
  2. Determine the upper boundary of the confidence interval by using x‾+2sn\bar{x}+\frac{2s}{\sqrt{n}}.
