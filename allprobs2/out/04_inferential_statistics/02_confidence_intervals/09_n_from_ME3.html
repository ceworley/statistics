    We can approximate a 95% confidence interval (where the 95% refers to the confidence level: how frequently these intervals straddle the population mean) by using x‾±2sn\bar{x}\pm \frac{2 s}{\sqrt{n}}. lower boundary=x‾−2sn\text{lower boundary} = \bar{x}-\frac{2s}{\sqrt{n}} upper boundary=x‾+2sn\text{upper boundary} = \bar{x}+\frac{2s}{\sqrt{n}} where x‾\bar{x} is the sample mean, ss is the sample standard deviation, and nn is the sample size.
