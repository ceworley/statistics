First, determine z⋆z^\star such that P(|Z|<z⋆)=0.5P(|Z|<z^\star)=0.5. It can help to draw a sketch.


The entire area under the density curve is 1. Thus, we can determine the areas of the tails (using symmetry). area of each tail=1−γ2=1−0.52=0.25\text{area of each tail} = \frac{1-\gamma}{2} = \frac{1-0.5}{2} = 0.25


Determine a leftward area with boundary z⋆z^\star. P(Z<z⋆)=γ+1−γ2=0.5+0.25=0.75P(Z<z^\star)=\gamma + \frac{1-\gamma}{2}= 0.5+0.25=0.75 It should be mentioned the expression can be simplified. P(Z<z⋆)=12+γ2=0.5+0.25=0.75P(Z<z^\star)= \frac{1}{2}+\frac{\gamma}{2}= 0.5+0.25=0.75


We rephrase the puzzle. We wish to determine z⋆z^\star such that P(Z<z⋆)=0.75P(Z<z^\star)=0.75. This is easy to determine using a zz-table, qnorm in R, norm.inv in spreadsheets, or other methods for evaluating the quantile function.

P(Z<0.67)=0.75P(Z<0.67)=0.75 In the original phrasing,

P(|Z|<0.67)=0.5P(|Z|<0.67)=0.5

So, z⋆=0.67z^\star = 0.67. Now, use the given expressions.

Lower bound=33.9−(0.67)(7.8)/206=33.5\text{Lower bound} = 33.9-(0.67)(7.8)/\sqrt{206} ~=~ 33.5 Upper bound=33.9+(0.67)(7.8)/206=34.3\text{Upper bound} = 33.9+(0.67)(7.8)/\sqrt{206} ~=~ 34.3

Spreadsheet

A spreadsheet can use the confidence.norm function. It takes three arguments:

  • Alpha (α\alpha) is the two-tail area, α=1−γ=1−0.5=0.5\alpha=1-\gamma=1-0.5=0.5
  • Population standard deviation, σ=7.8\sigma=7.8
  • Sample size, n=206n=206

The function returns the margin of error. margin of error=z⋆σn\text{margin of error} = \frac{z^\star \sigma}{\sqrt{n}}

So, in this case, if in a spreadsheet you typed =confidence.norm(1-0.5,7.8,206), the result would be the margin of error, 0.3665526. You then add/subtract the margin of error to/from the sample mean. x‾−ME=33.9−0.367=33.533\bar{x}-\text{ME} = 33.9-0.367 = 33.533 x‾+ME=33.9+0.367=34.267\bar{x}+\text{ME} = 33.9+0.367 = 34.267

You can do this with a spreadsheet.


R

xbar = 33.9
sigma = 7.8
n = 206
gamma = 0.5
zstar = qnorm(gamma+(1-gamma)/2)
ME = zstar*sigma/sqrt(n)
LB = xbar-ME
UB = xbar+ME
cat(sprintf("The lower bound: %.4f\nThe upper bound: %.4f",LB,UB))
