    Spreadsheet

    A spreadsheet can use the confidence.t function. It takes three arguments:

    • Alpha (α\alpha) is the two-tail area, α=1−γ=1−0.8=0.2\alpha=1-\gamma=1-0.8=0.2
    • sample standard deviation, s=7.8s=7.8
    • Sample size, n=43n=43

    The function returns the margin of error. margin of error=t⋆sn\text{margin of error} = \frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}

    So, in this case, if in a spreadsheet you typed =confidence.t(1-0.8,7.8,43), the result would be the margin of error, 1.5487567. You then add/subtract the margin of error to/from the sample mean. x‾−ME=33.9−1.549=32.351\bar{x}-\text{ME} = 33.9-1.549 = 32.351 x‾+ME=33.9+1.549=35.449\bar{x}+\text{ME} = 33.9+1.549 = 35.449

    You can do this with a spreadsheet.


    R

    xbar = 33.9
s = 7.8
n = 43
gamma = 0.8
df = n-1   # df is the degrees of freedom
tstar = qt(0.5+gamma/2, df)
ME = tstar*s/sqrt(n)
LB = xbar-ME
UB = xbar+ME
cat(sprintf("The lower bound: %.4f\nThe upper bound: %.4f",LB,UB))
