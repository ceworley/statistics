First, determine t⋆t^\star such that P(|T|<t⋆)=0.99P(|T|<t^\star)=0.99. It can help to draw a sketch.


The entire area under the density curve is 1. Thus, we can determine the areas of the tails (using symmetry). area of each tail=1−γ2=1−0.992=0.005\text{area of each tail} = \frac{1-\gamma}{2} = \frac{1-0.99}{2} = 0.005


Thus, we determine a left area. P(T<t⋆)=γ+1−γ2=0.99+0.005=0.995P(T<t^\star)=\gamma + \frac{1-\gamma}{2}= 0.99+0.005=0.995 It should be mentioned the expression can be simplified. P(T<t⋆)=12+γ2=0.5+0.495=0.995P(T<t^\star)= \frac{1}{2}+\frac{\gamma}{2}= 0.5+0.495=0.995


We rephrase the puzzle. We wish to determine t⋆t^\star such that P(T<t⋆)=0.995P(T<t^\star)=0.995. This is easy to determine using a tt-table, qt in R, t.inv in spreadsheets, or other methods for evaluating the quantile function.

P(T<3.25)=0.995P(T<3.25)=0.995 In the original phrasing,

P(|T|<3.25)=0.99P(|T|<3.25)=0.99

So, t⋆=3.25t^\star = 3.25. Now, use the given expressions.

Lower bound=26.6−(3.25)(7.5)/10=18.9\text{Lower bound} = 26.6-(3.25)(7.5)/\sqrt{10} ~=~ 18.9 Upper bound=26.6+(3.25)(7.5)/10=34.3\text{Upper bound} = 26.6+(3.25)(7.5)/\sqrt{10} ~=~ 34.3

Spreadsheet

A spreadsheet can use the confidence.t function. It takes three arguments:

  • Alpha (α\alpha) is the two-tail area, α=1−γ=1−0.99=0.01\alpha=1-\gamma=1-0.99=0.01
  • sample standard deviation, s=7.5s=7.5
  • Sample size, n=10n=10

The function returns the margin of error. margin of error=t⋆sn\text{margin of error} = \frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}

So, in this case, if in a spreadsheet you typed =confidence.t(1-0.99,7.5,10), the result would be the margin of error, 7.7076617. You then add/subtract the margin of error to/from the sample mean. x‾−ME=26.6−7.708=18.892\bar{x}-\text{ME} = 26.6-7.708 = 18.892 x‾+ME=26.6+7.708=34.308\bar{x}+\text{ME} = 26.6+7.708 = 34.308

You can do this with a spreadsheet.


R

xbar = 26.6
s = 7.5
n = 10
gamma = 0.99
df = n-1   # df is the degrees of freedom
tstar = qt(0.5+gamma/2, df)
ME = tstar*s/sqrt(n)
LB = xbar-ME
UB = xbar+ME
cat(sprintf("The lower bound: %.4f\nThe upper bound: %.4f",LB,UB))
