01_data_basics/01_counting/02_list_counting_between_v3

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

𝐱=52,55,54,59,55,56,57,57,53,58\mathbf{x} = 52, 55, 54, 59, 55, 56, 57, 57, 53, 58

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_between_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are inside 53.5 to 54.5? In other words, determine #[𝐱>53.5 𝙰𝙽𝙳 𝐱<54.5]\#[\mathbf{x}>53.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<54.5]. In other words, determine #[53.5<𝐱<54.5]\#[53.5<\mathbf{x}<54.5].
  2. How many of the measurements are outside 57.5 to 59.5? In other words, determine #[𝐱<57.5 𝙾𝚁 𝐱>59.5]\#[\mathbf{x}<57.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>59.5]. In other words, determine #[𝙽𝙾𝚃(57.5<𝐱<59.5)]\#[\texttt{NOT}(57.5<\mathbf{x}<59.5)].
  3. How many of the measurements are closer than 1 unit from 57.5? In other words, determine #[|𝐱−57.5|<1]\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-57.5\big|<1\right].
  4. How many of the measurements are farther than 3.5 units from 55? In other words, determine #[|𝐱−55|>3.5]\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-55\big|>3.5\right].
  5. Determine an integer radius rr of an interval with center 56.5 such that #[|𝐱−56.5|<r]=6\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-56.5\big|<r\right] ~=~ 6.
  6. Determine a half-integer radius rr of an interval with center 58 such that #[|𝐱−58|>r]=6\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-58\big|>r\right] ~=~ 6.
