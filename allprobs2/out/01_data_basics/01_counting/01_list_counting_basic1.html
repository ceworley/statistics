A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

𝐱=96,92,96,90,94,90,91,91,94,93\mathbf{x} = 96, 92, 96, 90, 94, 90, 91, 91, 94, 93

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_basic_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are less than 96? In other words, determine #[𝐱<96]\#[\mathbf{x}<96].
  2. How many of the measurements are less than or equal to 93? In other words, determine #[𝐱≤93]\#[\mathbf{x}\le93].
  3. How many of the measurements are greater than 96? In other words, determine #[𝐱>96]\#[\mathbf{x}>96].
  4. How many of the measurements are greater than or equal to 90? In other words, determine #[𝐱≥90]\#[\mathbf{x}\ge90].
  5. If 0 of the measurements are less than xcx_{\text{c}}, and xcx_{\text{c}} is a measurement from sample 𝐱\mathbf{x}, then what is xcx_{\text{c}}? In other words, determine a measurement xcx_{\text{c}} from sequence 𝐱\mathbf{x} such that #[𝐱<xc]=0\#[\mathbf{x}<x_{\text{c}}] = 0.
  6. If 5 of the measurements are less than or equal to xcx_{\text{c}}, and xcx_{\text{c}} is a measurement from sample 𝐱\mathbf{x}, then what is xcx_{\text{c}}? In other words, determine a measurement xcx_{\text{c}} from sequence 𝐱\mathbf{x} such that #[𝐱≤xc]=5\#[\mathbf{x}\le x_{\text{c}}] = 5.
  7. If 2 of the measurements are greater than xcx_{\text{c}}, and xcx_{\text{c}} is a measurement from sample 𝐱\mathbf{x}, then what is xcx_{\text{c}}? In other words, determine a measurement xcx_{\text{c}} from sequence 𝐱\mathbf{x} such that #[𝐱>xc]=2\#[\mathbf{x}>x_{\text{c}}] = 2.
  8. If 8 of the measurements are greater than or equal to xcx_{\text{c}}, and xcx_{\text{c}} is a measurement from sample 𝐱\mathbf{x}, then what is xcx_{\text{c}}? In other words, determine a measurement xcx_{\text{c}} from sequence 𝐱\mathbf{x} such that #[𝐱≥xc]=8\#[\mathbf{x}\ge x_{\text{c}}] = 8.
