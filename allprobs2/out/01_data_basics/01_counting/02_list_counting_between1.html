01_data_basics/01_counting/02_list_counting_between_v1

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

𝐱=94,97,91,92,97,92,93,91,95,95\mathbf{x} = 94, 97, 91, 92, 97, 92, 93, 91, 95, 95

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_between_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are inside 95.5 to 99.5? In other words, determine #[𝐱>95.5 𝙰𝙽𝙳 𝐱<99.5]\#[\mathbf{x}>95.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<99.5]. In other words, determine #[95.5<𝐱<99.5]\#[95.5<\mathbf{x}<99.5].
  2. How many of the measurements are outside 96.5 to 99.5? In other words, determine #[𝐱<96.5 𝙾𝚁 𝐱>99.5]\#[\mathbf{x}<96.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>99.5]. In other words, determine #[𝙽𝙾𝚃(96.5<𝐱<99.5)]\#[\texttt{NOT}(96.5<\mathbf{x}<99.5)].
  3. How many of the measurements are closer than 2 units from 96.5? In other words, determine #[|𝐱−96.5|<2]\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-96.5\big|<2\right].
  4. How many of the measurements are farther than 2 units from 96.5? In other words, determine #[|𝐱−96.5|>2]\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-96.5\big|>2\right].
  5. Determine an integer radius rr of an interval with center 96.5 such that #[|𝐱−96.5|<r]=4\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-96.5\big|<r\right] ~=~ 4.
  6. Determine a half-integer radius rr of an interval with center 93 such that #[|𝐱−93|>r]=6\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-93\big|>r\right] ~=~ 6.
