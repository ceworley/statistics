01_data_basics/02_proportion/02_list_proportion_between_v2

The lengths (in centimeters) of 10 lizards were recorded.

𝐱=13,12,14,18,15,14,14,13,19,13\mathbf{x} = 13, 12, 14, 18, 15, 14, 14, 13, 19, 13

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): lizard_data.csv


  1. What proportion of the measurements are inside 11.5 cm to 19.5 cm? In other words, determine prop[𝐱>11.5 𝙰𝙽𝙳 𝐱<19.5]\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>11.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<19.5]. In other words, determine prop[11.5<𝐱<19.5]\text{prop}[11.5<\mathbf{x}<19.5].
  2. What proportion of the measurements are outside 14.5 cm to 15.5 cm? In other words, determine prop[𝐱<14.5 𝙾𝚁 𝐱>15.5]\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<14.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>15.5]. In other words, determine prop[𝙽𝙾𝚃(14.5<𝐱<15.5)]\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(14.5<\mathbf{x}<15.5)].
  3. What proportion of the measurements are closer than 3.5 cm from 14 cm? In other words, determine prop[|𝐱−14|<3.5]\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-14\big|<3.5\right].
  4. What proportion of the measurements are farther than 1.5 cm from 15 cm? In other words, determine prop[|𝐱−15|>1.5]\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-15\big|>1.5\right].
  5. Determine a half-integer radius rr of an interval with center 17 cm such that prop[|𝐱−17|<r]=0.3\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-17\big|<r\right] ~=~ 0.3.
  6. Determine a half-integer radius rr of an interval with center 16 cm such that prop[|𝐱−16|>r]=0.9\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-16\big|>r\right] ~=~ 0.9.
