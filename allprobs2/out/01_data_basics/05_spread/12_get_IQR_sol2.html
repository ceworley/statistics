Method 1: method of medians

This method is described in the wikipedia page on IQR.

This method relies on first determining the size of each half.

  • If the sample size (nn) is even, divide the sample size by 2.
  • If the sample size (nn) is odd, first subtract 1, then divide the result by 2.

You determine the medians of the lowest half of the values and the highest half of the values. The IQR is the difference of those medians.

In this case, n=12n=12, so Q1Q_1 is the median of the lowest 6 numbers and Q3Q_3 is the median of the highest 6 numbers.

Method 1 by hand

Method 1 is easiest to do by hand.

sort(ùê±)=45.49,46.21,46.75,46.86,47.35,47.69,48.49,48.89,49.28,49.83,49.87,49.92\text{sort}(\mathbf{x}) = 45.49, 46.21, 46.75, 46.86, 47.35, 47.69, 48.49, 48.89, 49.28, 49.83, 49.87, 49.92

Because there are 12 values, the first quartile is the median of the lowest 6 values and the third quartile is the median of the highest 6 values.

Q1=median(45.49,46.21,46.75,46.86,47.35,47.69)=46.805Q_1 = \text{median}(45.49, 46.21, 46.75, 46.86, 47.35, 47.69) = 46.805 Q3=median(48.49,48.89,49.28,49.83,49.87,49.92)=49.555Q_3 = \text{median}(48.49, 48.89, 49.28, 49.83, 49.87, 49.92) = 49.555

The IQR is the difference between Q3Q_3 and Q1Q_1. IQR=49.555‚àí46.805=2.75\text{IQR} = 49.555-46.805 = 2.75

Method 1 with spreadsheet

Unfortunately, the built-in QUARTILE function does not use the method of medians (more about this in Method 2).

We sort the data, determine the median of the lowest 6 values, determine the median of the highest 6 values, and take a difference.

Method 1 with R

Again, the built-in function does not follow the method of medians. So, Method 1 is actually kind of difficult with R. The following code should be relatively easy to understand‚Ä¶ but it uses floor rounding, subsetting and the colon operator.

data = read.csv("get_IQR.csv")
x = data$x
n = length(x)
x_sorted = sort(x)
halfsize = floor(n/2) 
Q1 = median(x_sorted[1:halfsize])
Q3 = median(x_sorted[(n-halfsize+1):n])
iqr = Q3-Q1
print(iqr)
## [1] 2.75
