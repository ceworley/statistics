The key logical terms are “and”, “or”, and “given”. Notice that I am using “given” as a shorter version of “under the condition”.


  1. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{flower and white}]=\frac{41}{360}=0.114\)
  2. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{pig given pink}]=\frac{38}{95}=0.4\)
  3. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{pink given pig}]=\frac{38}{129}=0.295\)
  4. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{teal given flower}] = 0.117\) and \(\mathbb{P}[\text{teal given pig}] = 0.333\), so a pig is more likely to be teal than a flower is. / .
  5. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{white}]=\frac{105}{360}=0.292\)
  6. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{jigsaw or white}]=\frac{111+105-33}{360}=0.508\)
  7. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{jigsaw}]=\frac{111}{360}=0.308\)
