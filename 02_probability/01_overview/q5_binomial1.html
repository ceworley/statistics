02_probability/01_overview/q5_binomial_v1

In some situation, each trial has 0.66 probability of success. There will be 8 trials. (Thus the number of successes will follow a binomial distribution.)


  1. What is the probability of getting exactly 5 successes? In other words, determine \(\mathbb{P}[X = 5]\).
  2. What is the probability of getting exactly 6 successes? In other words, determine \(\mathbb{P}[X = 6]\).
  3. What is the probability of getting more than 6 successes? In other words, determine \(\mathbb{P}[X > 6]\).
  4. What is the probability of getting at least 6 successes? In other words, determine \(\mathbb{P}[X \ge 6]\).
  5. What is the probability of getting less than 6 successes? In other words, determine \(\mathbb{P}[X < 6]\).
  6. What is the probability of getting at most 6 successes? In other words, determine \(\mathbb{P}[X \le 6]\).
  7. Determine the mean number of successes.
  8. Determine the standard deviation of successes.
