The key logical terms are “and”, “or”, and “given”. Notice that I am using “given” as a shorter version of “under the condition”.


  1. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{bike given pink}]=\frac{16}{58}=0.276\)
  2. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{pink given bike}] = 0.11\) and \(\mathbb{P}[\text{pink given quilt}] = 0.216\), so a quilt is more likely to be pink than a bike is. / .
  3. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{pink}]=\frac{58}{415}=0.14\)
  4. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{cat}]=\frac{159}{415}=0.383\)
  5. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{orange given bike}]=\frac{30}{145}=0.207\)
  6. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{cat and violet}]=\frac{45}{415}=0.108\)
  7. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{cat or violet}]=\frac{159+76-45}{415}=0.458\)
