When Archie shoots archery, she records the horizontal position (\(x\)) and vertical position (\(y\)) of every arrow (in millimeters), using the bullseye as the origin.

From years of shooting, Archie has determined that \(X\) and \(Y\) are roughly bell-shaped with population standard deviations \(\sigma_x = 48\) and \(\sigma_y=42\) mm. However, Archie has a new sight, so her current population means (\(\mu_x\) and \(\mu_y\)) are unknown. (She hopes both are zero.)

Archie wants to get confidence intervals for \(\mu_x\) and \(\mu_y\), using a sample size \(n=12\) and a confidence level \(\gamma=0.99\). Whether or not a confidence interval straddles 0 will determine whether Archie adjusts that aspect of her sight.
